historyInfo1: Dhon Mendor's origins lie in conflict—a great feud with Dhon Toruhm drove ambitious dwarves to seek independence and establish their own realm. King Korg Ironheart led these rebels south and east, establishing settlements in new mountain ranges and founding a kingdom built on self-reliance and determination. What began as exile transformed into opportunity; freed from old constraints and rivalries, the settlers innovated rapidly, developing new crafting techniques and building a vibrant, independent civilization.
historyInfo2: Today, Dhon Mendor stands as a prosperous independent kingdom under continued Ironheart leadership. The four major clans—Ironheart, Graybeard, Stonefist, and Fleetfoot—each contribute unique strengths to the realm's governance and economy. The capital Gerond-Hul pulses with activity and innovation. Yet the memories of the original feud persist; relations with Dhon Toruhm remain tense, and Mendor dwarves maintain fierce independence, always conscious of their hard-won freedom and determined never to return to subordination.
constructionInfo1: Dhon Mendor is governed by King Korg Ironheart and a council representing the four founding clans: Ironheart (rulers and military), Graybeard (wisdom and diplomacy), Stonefist (craftsmanship and building), and Fleetfoot (trade and exploration). Leadership is based on merit and strength; each clan leader is chosen through trials and consensus, not simple succession. The system ensures diverse perspectives in governance while maintaining clear authority. The kingdom values innovation and adaptation, quickly adopting new ideas that benefit the realm.
constructionInfo2: Key figures include King Korg Ironheart (strong founder and current leader), clan leaders representing each of the four founding families, war captains who have earned respect through deeds in battle, and master craftsmen whose innovations define the realm's identity. These leaders balance fierce independence with practical cooperation, maintaining Dhon Mendor's prosperity while remaining vigilant against external threats and internal challenges.
locationInfo1: Gerond-Hul, the capital with substantial population and thriving commerce, stands as Dhon Mendor's heart. The city is a showcase of innovation—its architecture combines functionality with artistry, its forges produce exceptional quality goods, and its markets draw traders from across Amra. The city pulses with energy and ambition; younger dwarves eager to make their mark and experienced leaders building for future generations work side by side. The city represents Dhon Mendor's core value: that independence and innovation can overcome any challenge.
locationInfo2: Dhon Mendor contains sites of both accomplishment and mystery: the City of the Thousand Spires (architectural marvel with defensive capabilities), The King's Forge (royal crafting center producing regalia and ceremonial weapons), The Shadow Mines (deep excavations yielding rare materials and occasionally disturbing discoveries), The Mountain Pass (trade route and strategic location), and the Ruins of Arvandorak (ancient site predating current kingdom, holding unexplained mysteries and occasionally dangerous discoveries).

title: Gerond-Hul
historyInfo1: Gerond-Hul is Dhon Mendor's vibrant capital, a city of innovation and ambition where the four founding clans work together toward common prosperity. The city showcases dwarven craftsmanship at its finest, with architecture that balances beauty and function. Markets overflow with goods from local craftsmen and distant traders; forges produce weapons, tools, and art of exceptional quality; and the city's academies attract scholars seeking knowledge of new techniques and theories.
historyInfo2: The city represents Dhon Mendor's core identity—that hard work, innovation, and cooperation can overcome any obstacle. Young dwarves seek opportunity here, while experienced leaders build for future generations. Gerond-Hul pulses with energy and purpose, a testament to what independent dwarves can achieve when freed from constraint and united by shared vision.

title: City of the Thousand Spires
historyInfo1: The City of the Thousand Spires is an architectural marvel where towers rise densely from the mountainside, each crafted with precision and beauty. The spires serve both aesthetic and defensive purposes, their arrangement creating sightlines for guards and their height providing psychological dominance. The city is a showcase of what dwarven innovation and ambition can achieve.
historyInfo2: The city is renowned throughout Amra for its beauty and its formidable defenses. The city represents Dhon Mendor's determination to build something great—something that endures through beauty as well as strength.

title: The Shadow Mines
historyInfo1: The Shadow Mines are deep excavations beneath Dhon Mendor, yielding rare materials essential to the kingdom's economy and crafting. The mines reach to impressive depths, with multiple levels of tunnels and chambers. The deeper sections are dangerous—stability becomes uncertain, and miners sometimes encounter strange phenomena or disturbing discoveries from ages past.
historyInfo2: The mines hold both treasure and danger. Each new level brings potential for valuable ore discovery and risk of encountering ancient traps or creatures. Some miners whisper of hearing strange sounds from the deepest levels or of discovering ancient worked stone that predates dwarven civilization. The Shadow Mines remain a source of both wealth and mystery for Dhon Mendor.

title: The King's Forge
historyInfo1: The King's Forge is Dhon Mendor's most prestigious crafting center, where the finest and most important items are created. Here, the greatest master smiths work under royal commission, creating weapons, armor, and regalia of exceptional quality and often magical properties. The forge is open only to those with royal permission or special need.
historyInfo2: Items forged here are legendary—a weapon from the King's Forge is a status symbol and a guarantee of quality. The forge represents Dhon Mendor's commitment to excellence and its pride in dwarven craftsmanship. Occasionally, adventurers are tasked with retrieving rare materials for the forge or protecting important commissions.
