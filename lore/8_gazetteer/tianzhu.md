historyInfo1: Tianzhu is an ancient eastern empire with roots stretching back beyond recorded time, governed by a complex family-based aristocracy that prizes harmony, ancestral wisdom, and the balance of cosmic forces. Legend tells of the Warrior Emperor who emerged from nomadic tribes to unite the scattered, warring peoples of Tianzhu under a single banner. The Warrior Emperor possessed unmatched martial prowess, keen intellect, and indomitable spirit that inspired loyalty among the tribes. His struggles against formidable enemies—the Yuan Clan with their beastmasters, the Qiang tribe with their cavalry mastery, and the Shadow Walkers with their arcane arts—forged the foundation of the Zhou Dynasty. Each victory added new strength to the emerging empire, paid for with blood and sweat and tested against every form of opposition.
historyInfo2: Today, Tianzhu stands as a vibrant testament to its past triumphs and enduring spirit. The Zhou Dynasty maintains rule through a delicate balance of power shared with six great families—the Liang, Wu, Liu, Song, Zhu, and Hu—each commanding vast territories, resources, and military strength. This equilibrium has prevented any single family from dominating while maintaining the empire's stability and prosperity. Beneath the official structures of governance, the Blood Tigers—a secret society wielding influence through espionage, intelligence, and covert operations—shape events from the shadows. In Tianzhu, family loyalty supersedes all other bonds; marriage alliances forge and break political coalitions; succession disputes can trigger regional conflicts. The empire thrives on this complex balance—dangerous yet dynamic, rigid yet adaptive.
constructionInfo1: Emperor Zhen Zhou rules with wisdom and benevolence, committed to prosperity and stability. His authority is tempered by the necessity of maintaining harmony with the six great families, each of whom possesses significant power and resources. The empire is governed through councils of family representatives, regional governors appointed by family interests, and imperial bureaucracies staffed by merit-selected scholars and administrators. The Liang family, possessed of significant influence over military and economic affairs, subtly erodes Zhou control through the slow machinations of the Jade Phoenix faction. The Blood Tigers gnaw away at imperial roots with dark magic and abhorrent practices, infiltrating the deepest corners of the Celestial Court.
constructionInfo2: The great families maintain their own armies, trade networks, and intelligence operations. The Wu family dominates maritime trade; the Liu family manages intellectual and spiritual institutions; the Song family oversees resource extraction; the Zhu family controls manufacturing; and the Hu family oversees internal security. Crown Prince Yun Zhou, heir to the throne, navigates the dangerous world of court intrigue while his sister Princess Lili Zhou, a renowned scholar and poet, serves as a valuable advisor. Grand Vizier Ming Zhou, though appearing loyal to the Emperor, pursues his own ambitious agenda, making him a dangerous player in ongoing power struggles.
locationInfo1: Cathai, the capital, is a sprawling metropolis of imperial grandeur, glittering like a jewel encrusted in mountainside and mirrored in the tranquil Copper Lake below. The city is built around the Celestial Court—the imperial palace complex where the Zhou family governs the empire. Cathai contains vast bureaucratic institutions housing tens of thousands of officials, scholars, and administrators; temples devoted to ancestors and cosmic principles; academies dedicated to education and advancement; and markets overflowing with goods from across the empire and beyond. The city is organized around the principle of cosmic harmony, with architecture reflecting celestial patterns and hierarchies mirroring the natural order.
locationInfo2: Beyond Cathai lie six major cities representing the great families' primary power centers: Jechon, center of trade and commerce; Qushu, home to intellectual and spiritual pursuits; Bisai, nexus of military power; Manju, hub of resource management and manufacturing; Shigon, seat of cultural achievement; and Yeosu, center of regional governance. The Copper Lake at Tianzhu's heart contains valuable ore deposits, particularly copper, whose wealth has financed imperial endeavors. The Eternal Walls stretch from the Lake of Swords in the north to the Tail of the Dragon mountains in the south, built by Emperor Shi after the hard-fought victory at Dragon Plain against the orcish horde of Warchief Skarr.

title: Cathai
historyInfo1: Cathai has been the seat of imperial power for untold centuries, a city of breathtaking scale and architectural magnificence. The Celestial Court stands at its heart—an enormous complex of interconnected palaces, halls, and gardens where the Zhou family conducts the business of empire. The city is organized around the principle of cosmic harmony, with architecture reflecting celestial patterns and bureaucratic hierarchies mirroring the natural order. Vast institutions process imperial affairs; academies nurture talent through merit-based selection; temples honor ancestors and cosmic principles. The markets teem with goods from across the empire and beyond; theaters present performances celebrating Tianzhu's achievements; scholars debate philosophy and governance in academies.
historyInfo2: Cathai is a city of ceremonies and protocols where every action carries significance and hierarchy determines access. Yet beneath the order lies constant maneuvering between families seeking advantage, with the Liang family's slow erosion of Zhou control, the Blood Tigers' dark operations, and courtiers plotting under veils of courteous smiles and deferential bows. The city embodies Tianzhu's contradictions—magnificent yet constrained, prosperous yet tense, harmonious yet competitive.

The temple's nine towers represent each dragon, their architecture reflecting celestial principles and cosmic balance. The temple serves as both a place of spiritual devotion and a center of philosophical learning where monks study the relationship between cosmic order and human governance.

title: The Jade Tower
historyInfo1: The Jade Tower stands at Cathai's heart as an architectural marvel and symbol of imperial power, built from pale jade stone and rising many stories into the sky. The tower is visible from great distances, serving as a beacon of imperial authority. The tower houses imperial treasuries, archives containing the empire's historical records, and private chambers where the emperor conducts sensitive imperial business.
historyInfo2: The Jade Tower is closely guarded and accessible only to those with imperial authorization. Within its walls lie some of the empire's greatest treasures—gold, jewels, ancient artifacts, and sacred relics. The tower contains libraries holding imperial records and secret documents related to empire governance and the operations of the Blood Tigers.

title: The Celestial Court
historyInfo1: The Celestial Court is the imperial palace complex at Cathai's heart, the seat of the Zhou family's power and the center of imperial governance. The complex is enormous, containing hundreds of buildings, gardens, halls, and chambers organized around principles of cosmic harmony. The innermost sanctum is the Chamber of Ultimate Harmony, where the emperor conducts the empire's most sensitive affairs and where cosmic rituals believed to sustain imperial power are performed.
historyInfo2: The Celestial Court is heavily guarded and accessible only to those with imperial authorization. Within its walls, the great families negotiate their complex alliances, the Blood Tigers conduct their covert operations, and the emperor grapples with challenges of maintaining harmony among competing interests. The court embodies both the grandeur of imperial power and the complex limitations imposed by the need to maintain balance among the great families.

historyInfo1: The Ruins of the Lost City of Qingzang are all that remain of an ancient metropolis that mysteriously fell into decline centuries ago. The ruins are a source of fascination for scholars seeking to understand the empire's distant past and for adventurers seeking ancient treasures and artifacts.

The mountain is considered sacred, believed to be the dwelling place of solar deities that influence the empire's destiny. The mountain is a destination for pilgrimage and spiritual practice, with temples and monasteries built on its slopes where monks study cosmic principles and seek enlightenment.
