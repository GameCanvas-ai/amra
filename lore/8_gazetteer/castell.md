callout1: Castell was founded through the Accords of the Broken Spears, uniting elves, dwarves, and humans in an unprecedented compact.
callout2: The rising power of the Eothas Inquisition threatens the delicate balance that once held the kingdom together.
historyInfo1: Three centuries ago, the elves of Tel'Dovan faced an impossible choice. Plagued by monstrosities from the Peaks of the Draken, they lacked the population to defend and govern their lands adequately. At the Capitulations of the Hundred Nights, the elven kingdoms proposed ceding territories to form a new human kingdom under elven and dwarven guidance. When Tel'Areth rejected the accord, the elves of Tel'Dovan turned to their ancient allies, the dwarves of Dhon Toruhm, who recommended House Dawnspear of Aregor as partners. Thus was born the Accords of the Broken Spears, a triumvirate in which Dawnspear humans, elven masters of magic from Tel'Dovan, and dwarven masters of defense from Dhon Toruhm would rule together in harmony and mutual respect.
historyInfo2: The Dawnspears founded Castela as their capital and built a kingdom founded on honor, duty, and justice upheld by Ethoras. For generations, humans, elves, and dwarves labored together, constructing cities, revitalizing ancient ruins, and forging trade routes. Yet as the clergy of Ethoras swelled in power and influence, they began advocating for an inquisition—a dark turn that even King Alejandro, growing aged and weaker, eventually permitted. Now the Eothas Inquisition wields exceptional authority, persecuting those deemed heretical, sowing fear and resentment among the populace. External threats from Tel'Areth and the Peaks of the Draken continue to escalate, while internal tensions between noble houses threaten to tear Castell apart. Prince Caden Dawnspear offers hope for change, yet dissidents whisper of alternative rulers and radical transformation.
constructionInfo1: Castell is governed through the Triumvirate of Castell, where House Dawnspear (human rulers) share authority with elven Masters of Magic from Tel'Dovan and dwarven Masters of Defense from Dhon Toruhm. The kingdom comprises eight major noble houses vying for power and influence: Dawnspear (ruling family), Castramare (northern border defenders), Palbarde (steppe guardians), Wolfclaw (royal executors), Dragonsbane (southern defenders against the Peaks), Centos (northern scouts and fighters), Moonsport (seafarers), and House of Castros (lesser nobility). The balance of power shifts constantly as the Eothas Inquisition exerts growing influence, some houses question the Dawnspears' fitness to rule, and ancient threats press ever harder on Castell's borders.
constructionInfo2: Key figures include King Alejandro Dawnspear (aging, wise yet weakened), Prince Caden Dawnspear (charismatic heir offering hope for reform), Duke Rodrigo Castramare (shrewd, critical of crown authority), Count Gregor Palbarde (just and pragmatic), Duke Raynard Wolfclaw (fearless and loyal), Lady Evelyn Dragonsbane (brave protector of the south), Lord Malcolm Centos (skilled warrior and loyal ally), Master Aeron Silverglade (elven master of magic), and Master Thorin Ironfist (dwarven master of defenses). These leaders embody their houses' values while navigating treacherous politics and escalating external pressures.
locationInfo1: Castela, the capital of Castell, stands as the heart of the kingdom's government and culture. The city is built upon the ruins of an ancient elven settlement and reflects the collaborative architecture of three peoples. Marble and stone buildings blend elven elegance with dwarven solidity and human innovation. The Triumvirate's council chamber overlooks the city from a central citadel, while temples to Ethoras rise prominently throughout the streets. Markets bustle with merchants from across Amra; scholars and mages study in academies founded on the Dawnspears' commitment to knowledge. Yet the city also bears the iron fist of the Inquisition—watch towers, templars in the streets, and an undercurrent of fear mark the transformation from sanctuary to something darker.
locationInfo2: Castell's notable places reflect its diverse heritage and mounting tensions. The Rainbow Tower, an elven marvel of magic and craftsmanship, stands as a symbol of Tel'Dovan's wisdom. The Cave of the Stars, sacred to both elves and humans, harbors ancient knowledge. The Temple of Erio honors a forgotten god and holds mysteries from Castell's founding. The Ruins of Elvandar whisper of an earlier elven civilization. Catacombs of the Dead contain archives of the kingdom's history. Lake of the Moons reflects both stars and secrets. Valley of Shadows harbors dangers and forbidden magic. Zalanthar's Tower looms as a mysterious arcane stronghold. The fortress city of Trizarbo guards the volatile northern border with Tel'Areth, while DarkGate defends the south against the Peaks of the Draken.

title: House Dawnspear
group: Noble Houses
historyInfo1: House Dawnspear emerged from bold explorers and traders whose encounters with the dwarves of Dhon Toruhm forged bonds of honor and mutual respect. Richard Dawnspear's pioneering expedition opened channels of knowledge and trade that benefited both peoples. Growing dissatisfied with the intrigues and extravagances of Aregor's court, the Dawnspears were drawn to the elves of Tel'Dovan's vision of a new kingdom built on values of honor, duty, and justice. With elven blessing and dwarven support, they founded Castell and established the Triumvirate, proving themselves wise stewards of a diverse realm.
historyInfo2: For centuries, the Dawnspears ruled with wisdom and compassion, earning respect from subjects and allies. Yet in King Alejandro's twilight years, the clergy of Ethoras ascended to dominance, establishing an inquisition that has brought oppression and control. Prince Caden offers promise of reform, but dissenters among the nobility openly question whether the Dawnspears can reclaim their original ideals. As King Alejandro weakens, unable to act decisively against mounting threats from Tel'Areth and the Peaks of the Draken, the kingdom teeters on the precipice of transformation—or collapse.

title: House Castramare
group: Noble Houses
historyInfo1: House Castramare was established to guard Trizarbo, a strategic stronghold on the volatile northern border where the elves of Tel'Areth constantly threaten incursion. Founder Roderick Castramare proved an exceptional leader, repelling numerous attacks and earning the respect of both crown and dwarven allies. The house developed exceptional diplomatic and military skills, maintaining precarious peace on Castell's most dangerous frontier by balancing relations between Tel'Areth, Tel'Dovan, and Dhon Toruhm.
historyInfo2: Yet as the Eothas Inquisition's oppressive grip tightens and discontent spreads through Castell, House Castramare has grown bold in questioning Dawnspear leadership. While no concrete evidence of conspiracy exists, rumors swirl that they may be plotting to expand their power and weaken the royal family. Their growing influence at court and their vocal criticisms have made them a focal point of hope for reform—or a destabilizing force that could tear the kingdom apart.

title: House Palbarde
group: Noble Houses
historyInfo1: House Palbarde, the Lords of Stonekeep, maintain a lower profile in court intrigues, choosing instead to focus on protecting their steppe lands and managing trade with regional kingdoms. Founded to safeguard trade caravans and control bandits, they have expanded Stonekeep into an imposing fortress dominating the steppe. Unlike more ambitious houses, the Palbardes balance their loyalty to the Dawnspears with pragmatic focus on the well-being of their lands and subjects.
historyInfo2: Though loyal vassal to House Dawnspear, the Palbardes have expressed private concerns about the Eothas Inquisition's oppression and its effects on stability and prosperity. They represent a balance of power in Castell politics—not openly allied with critics like the Castramares, yet harboring reservations about the current direction. Their influence could prove decisive should they ever decide to abandon neutrality.

title: House Wolfclaw
group: Noble Houses
historyInfo1: House Wolfclaw has been unwaveringly loyal executors of the Dawnspear will since Castell's founding. From their headquarters in Maentra, they have used strategic position and political acumen to protect Castell's interests, serve as the eyes and ears of the crown at court, and crush any threat to royal authority. They are masters of both battlefield prowess and court intrigue, orchestrating political moves that have strengthened the Dawnspears through difficult times.
historyInfo2: Despite rising tensions and the Eothas Inquisition's dark influence, House Wolfclaw maintains steadfast loyalty to the Dawnspears. Yet they remain aware of other houses' concerns and attentive to shifts in power. As executors of royal will, they will continue to play a crucial role in Castell's politics and defense, fighting to maintain the stability and prosperity they value.

title: House Dragonsbane
group: Noble Houses
historyInfo1: House Dragonsbane, headquartered at DarkGate, has borne the primary burden of defending southern Castell against the monstrosities spilling forth from the Peaks of the Draken for centuries. This position has cost them dearly—countless fallen members, endless sacrifice, unwavering vigilance against escalating threats. Yet the family's reputation as protectors and their commitment to duty have earned respect and inspired many.
historyInfo2: In recent years, the Dragonsbane have noted with mounting resentment that the Dawnspears seem less committed to southern defense, sending fewer reinforcements as the Eothas Inquisition gains influence. Their sense of abandonment, combined with their reputation for sacrifice and bravery, has made them an ideal candidate in the eyes of many who seek alternatives to Dawnspear rule. Secret alliances and conspiracies have begun forming around them, raising the possibility that House Dragonsbane could emerge as challengers to the crown.

title: House Centos
group: Noble Houses
historyInfo1: House Centos, known for martial skill and the breeding of formidable giant warhounds, holds the central northern steppes and serves as stalwart vassals to the Wolfclaw. Since Castell's founding, they have stood as a bulwark against northern aggression. Their expert scouts are legendary for tracking enemies across winter landscapes, and their rangers are renowned for endurance in the harshest conditions.
historyInfo2: Even amid escalating Inquisition influence and noble house tensions, House Centos maintains steadfast allegiance to the Wolfclaw and Dawnspears. Yet beneath this loyalty, internal struggles persist. The harsh climate and constant resource scarcity have bred a pragmatic people who value strength and adaptability above all else. This pragmatism could prove double-edged if circumstances change.

title: Castela
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: Castela, the capital of Castell and seat of the Triumvirate, rises as a city blending the architectural styles and sensibilities of three peoples. Built upon ancient elven ruins, the city's marble and stone buildings reflect collaborative design—elven elegance married to dwarven solidity and human innovation. The central citadel houses the Triumvirate's council chamber overlooking the city, a symbol of shared governance.
historyInfo2: Castela is a center of learning and commerce, with academies, libraries, temples to Ethoras, and markets bustling with merchants from across Amra. Yet the city also bears the mark of the Inquisition—templars patrol streets, inquisitorial towers loom over neighborhoods, and an undercurrent of fear pervades daily life. The city that once embodied hope for unity now feels trapped between its noble past and a darker future, its transformation from sanctuary to something sinister evident to all who walk its streets.

title: Ruins of Elvandar
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Ruins of Elvandar, scattered across eastern Castell, are remnants of an earlier elven civilization that predates even Tel'Dovan's founding. The stones speak of advanced magic and architecture lost to time and conflict. Scholars and adventurers come seeking to unlock secrets hidden within the crumbling structures, searching for artifacts and knowledge that might illuminate Castell's distant past.
historyInfo2: Yet these ruins are not merely archaeological sites—they pulse with residual magical power from ages long forgotten. Some say that spirits of ancient elves linger here, and that certain artifacts hidden within possess power sufficient to reshape kingdoms. The ruins have become a focus of interest for various factions within Castell, each seeking advantage through discovery of Elvandar's lost treasures.

title: Rainbow Tower
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Rainbow Tower stands as an elven marvel of magic and craftsmanship, a structure that seems to defy natural law. Its crystalline surface refracts light into a constant display of color that can be seen for miles across the surrounding countryside. The tower serves as both a symbol of Tel'Dovan's magical prowess and a beacon of hope for those who cherish the original vision of Castell's Triumvirate.
historyInfo2: Within the tower's walls, elven mages study the deepest mysteries of arcane magic, conduct research that benefits all three peoples, and maintain libraries of knowledge accumulated over centuries. The tower is sacred space for those who believe in Castell's founding ideals, and it has become a refuge for those fleeing the Inquisition's persecution. Yet as tensions escalate, the tower's neutrality is increasingly threatened.

title: Cave of the Stars
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Cave of the Stars is a sacred cavern that holds significance for both elves and humans, containing murals and inscriptions from Castell's earliest days. The cave's ceiling is studded with crystals that catch light and shimmer like stars, creating an ethereal atmosphere. Pilgrims and seekers come here to meditate, seek wisdom, and commune with the land's ancient spirits.
historyInfo2: The cave is believed to hold knowledge of Castell's true founding and the original intentions of the Triumvirate. Some say that within its deepest chambers lie objects of power from the age of the Dawnspears' alliance with the elves and dwarves. The cave represents a spiritual anchor for those who wish to restore Castell to its noble origins.

title: Catacombs of the Dead
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Catacombs of the Dead lie beneath Castela, serving as burial place for Castell's nobility and a repository for historical archives spanning the kingdom's three-century existence. The catacombs are vast, with passages that descend deep into the earth, chambers filled with sarcophagi, and walls covered in inscriptions recording family histories, political treaties, and pivotal moments.
historyInfo2: The catacombs are guarded and restricted, yet rumors speak of secret passages, hidden chambers, and documents that could expose conspiracies or reveal forgotten knowledge. Some whisper that the remains of the original founders of Castell rest here, blessed or cursed with power that might grant victory to whoever claims them. The catacombs represent both the kingdom's past and the secrets it keeps.

title: Valley of Shadows
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Valley of Shadows, located in the western reaches of Castell, is a place of dark reputation and mysterious danger. The valley's geography creates perpetual twilight within, shadows that seem to move of their own accord, and a pervasive sense of unease. Few venture here willingly, and fewer still emerge unchanged from the experience.
historyInfo2: The valley harbors forbidden magic from ages past, creatures that should not exist, and forces that predate Castell itself. Yet it also contains artifacts and knowledge that could prove invaluable to those bold or desperate enough to seek them. Some believe the valley is a scar left by the ancient war between elves and demons, a place where reality itself has been wounded.

title: DarkGate Fortress
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: DarkGate stands as Castell's southern fortress, built into a mountainous pass that leads toward the Peaks of the Draken. The fortress is formidable, designed to withstand siege and assault, its walls thick enough to resist dragon fire. It serves as House Dragonsbane's stronghold and the primary defense against the monstrosities that constantly threaten from the mountains.
historyInfo2: DarkGate has seen countless battles, its stone darkened by blood and fire, its defenders hardened by centuries of conflict. The fortress is both a symbol of Castell's resilience and a grim reminder of the relentless threats it faces. From DarkGate, patrols venture into the Peaks seeking to contain threats before they can reach the heart of the kingdom, and it is here that the Dragonsbane have earned their legendary reputation.
