historyInfo1: In ages past, the fierce and chaotic tribes of Alkebu's vast deserts warred without end. The tribal leader Kefilwe Nalutalo rose from among them, a warrior of courage and vision. After a perilous quest to a sacred mountain, Kefilwe made a pact with the djinni king: in exchange for their aid in building a magnificent oasis city, the tribes would protect and honor the spirits of the air. With djinni magic, they transformed barren sands into fertile paradise, establishing an irrigation system that sustained generations.
historyInfo2: Yet even prosperity breeds tension. The dao—spirits of the earth—grew envious of the alliance and unleashed the War of the Sands, a catastrophic conflict of earthquakes and tempests. Kefilwe, aged but wise, negotiated peace with the dao queen, forging a new accord that united all parties. Today, Alkebu thrives as a melting pot where ancient wisdom and modern ambition coexist—but internal rivalries between clans and external threats from monsters and ambitious powers test the region's fragile balance.
constructionInfo1: Alkebu is governed by five principal clans, each commanding territory and loyalty: the Burning Sun Clan (martial and devoted to the sun deity), the Black Lion Clan (fierce warriors driven by glory), the Blue Moon Clan (scholarly and nature-revering), the Golden Snake Clan (merchants and traders), and the Dragon Legacy Clan (descendants of the ancient Dragonblood, keepers of draconic honor). The capital, Anathol, is ruled by a council of tribal leaders elected by their people and meeting in the Great Citadel of Tarses. Power shifts constantly as clans vie for influence through commerce, military strength, and political cunning.
constructionInfo2: Key figures include Maya Amanar (leader of the Burning Sun Clan, known for wisdom and piety), Nyala Bolawa (celebrated warrior of the Black Lion Clan), Amara Lunal (sorceress of the Blue Moon Clan and member of the Seekers of Twilight), Kemet Clever-Eye (ambitious merchant of the Golden Snake Clan), and Sora Bronzewings (priestess of the Dragon Legacy Clan). These leaders embody their clans' values yet sometimes pursue conflicting agendas, creating both alliances and tensions.
locationInfo1: Anathol, the gleaming heart of Alkebu, rises as a jewel of white marble and commerce where 35,000 souls of diverse races dwell in harmony. The Great Plaza bustles with merchants hawking exotic goods and performers dazzling crowds; above it looms the Great Citadel of Tarses, whose walls depict Alkebu's history in magnificent murals and mosaics. The city's port—guarded by a fortress of stone and watchtowers—thrives with ships crossing the Sand Sea, making Anathol a beacon of trade and culture where ancient tradition and modern enterprise dance together in the desert sun.
locationInfo2: Beyond Anathol lies a realm of wonders and perils. The mystical Ivory Citadel houses the Blue Moon Clan's library and the legendary Eye of Anaya, an artifact of immense power. The Market of the Thousand Lights glimmers with rare goods and magical wonders. The Dragon Fang Mountains shelter fierce predators and ancient treasures. Scattered oases—Menaka's spiritual temples, Cerenan's fishing harbors, Kupa's fortress walls—each tell their own tales of Alkebu's vast and storied tapestry.

title: Burning Sun Clan
group: Clans
historyInfo1: The Burning Sun Clan worships Ethoras, the sun deity, as a divine source of life and strength. These devoted people see in the sun's embrace not cruelty but divine purpose. For generations, the mighty Amanar royal family has guided the clan's warriors to become legendary champions of courage and martial prowess. From youth, these warriors train in the desert arts of war, preparing to defend their people and sacred traditions. The clan has produced master artisans whose weapons, jewelry, and textiles blend utility with sunlit devotion.
historyInfo2: Masait, the clan's capital, gleams like a pearl amid dunes and oases. Its salt industry and fishing grounds sustain the population while its annual salt fair draws merchants from across Amra. Yet the Burning Sun Clan faces mounting pressure: envious rivals covet their resources, monsters threaten their borders, and ambitious leaders quietly scheme against the delicate balance of power. The clan's unwavering faith in the sun guides them still, but shadows grow longer on the horizon.

title: Black Lion Clan
group: Clans
historyInfo1: The Black Lion Clan embodies the warrior spirit. From childhood, their people train in combat with sword and spear as naturally as they breathe. War and conquest are not merely means to an end but a way of life—their motto, "For Glory and Victory," echoes through the fortress city of Kupa. Yet beneath the love of battle lies tactical brilliance; the clan's leaders are master strategists who outwit opponents with cunning precision, striking fear into enemy hearts before blades even cross.
historyInfo2: Kupa, perched upon a hill like a sentinel, houses the clan's elite warriors and training schools that produce mercenaries sought throughout the land. The city excels in metalwork, forging weapons of deadly beauty, while its famous beer draws enthusiasts from distant lands. Though the Black Lion Clan's reputation for martial prowess is unchallenged, their thirst for glory sometimes overreaches, making enemies of former allies and testing the bonds that hold Alkebu together.

title: Blue Moon Clan
group: Clans
historyInfo1: The Blue Moon Clan dwells in Cerenan, nestled amid mountains and intricate gardens that speak to their reverence for nature and the mystical arts. Members of the clan dedicate themselves to scholarly pursuits, studying ancient scrolls and arcane knowledge to unlock the universe's secrets. They perceive in all things—trees, streams, rocks—the presence of the sacred and guard the natural world as a holy duty. This reverence for balance and interconnection grants them remarkable foresight, enabling them to prophesy and advise other clans with wisdom born of deep contemplation.
historyInfo2: Yet the Blue Moon Clan's spiritual focus does not render them powerless. Talented sorceresses and druids wield magic with precision, protecting their lands and people. The Ivory Citadel stands as their greatest treasure—a library of magical tomes and the home of the legendary Eye of Anaya. As the clan's wisdom is increasingly sought in times of political turmoil, their peaceful nature is tested by those who would exploit their knowledge or steal their secrets.

title: Golden Snake Clan
group: Clans
historyInfo1: The Golden Snake Clan rules the opulent city of Jafari through commerce and cunning negotiation. These shrewd merchants control vast trade networks, amassing wealth that rivals the treasures of kingdoms. Masters of persuasion and alliance-building, they use business acumen to secure their dominance in Alkebu's economic landscape. Yet the clan does not rely solely on wealth; they are also skilled manipulators, able to convince others to do their bidding through charm and calculated leverage.
historyInfo2: For all their focus on trade and material prosperity, the Golden Snake Clan knows how to enjoy the fruits of their labor. Lavish banquets, exotic fashions, and exquisite jewelry mark their celebrations, while their merchants finance military ventures to protect Alkebu's broader interests. However, their relentless pursuit of wealth occasionally blinds them to ethical concerns, and some members have descended into shadowy enterprises and dark alliances to further their gains.

title: Dragon Legacy Clan
group: Clans
historyInfo1: The Dragon Legacy Clan traces its lineage to the ancient Dragonblood, warriors created by dragons to fight in the Black Blood War. After suffering devastating losses, the Dragonblood fled south and founded their own domain under the brave leader Rorik, constructing the impregnable Dragon's Keep as their fortress. The dragon's blood flowing through their veins is said to grant them strength, power, and unwavering determination. Today, the clan honors their draconic heritage through martial tradition, ancestor worship, and sacrifices to the dragon gods.
historyInfo2: Though their stronghold lies in a remote mountain region, the Dragon Legacy Clan wields significant influence in Alkebu's politics and economy. Many leaders from other houses seek their alliance, viewing them as forces of tradition and honor in an ever-changing world. Yet darker currents stir within the clan—ambitious schemers pursue forbidden knowledge, and some members have fallen under the spell of sinister factions that promise power at any cost.

title: Anathol
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: Anathol, a city of 35,000 souls, stands as the beating heart of Alkebu and the seat of the council of tribal leaders. Its white marble buildings and sculptures of legendary heroes inspire awe; the Great Plaza bustles with merchants, performers, and pilgrims. The imposing Great Citadel of Tarses crowns a hilltop overlooking the city, its walls adorned with murals depicting Alkebu's history and struggles. Here, elected tribal leaders meet to make decisions that shape the region's destiny.
historyInfo2: Anathol's port is a crucial nexus of maritime commerce, with ships arriving from across the Sand Sea and beyond. The city's markets overflow with exotic goods, spices, and rare wares. Its festivals—particularly the Solstice Festival, a three-day celebration of music, dance, and bonfires—demonstrate the vibrant cultural synthesis of Alkebu's diverse peoples. From the Great Plaza to the fortress-guarded harbor, Anathol pulses with the energy of a civilization where trade, tradition, and political intrigue thrive in delicate balance.

title: Red Sand Desert
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Red Sand Desert bears the scar of catastrophic magic. Once a lush and thriving land, it was transformed into a magical wasteland when the sorceress Zephyra Scorchsong summoned a genie to turn the tide of the Black Blood War. In exchange for her soul, the genie cursed the entire region with red sands that shimmer with residual magical power. Victory came at an unbearable cost: the Night Wind clan vanished, and the desert became a place of deadly enchantment and forbidden knowledge.
historyInfo2: Today, the Red Sand Desert remains feared and forbidden by many. Brave adventurers venture into its depths seeking ancient relics and forgotten temples rumored to hold untold power. Yet danger lurks at every turn: potent magic, treacherous terrain, and deadly traps await the unwary. Some witches and sorcerers from the Night Wind's remnants attempt to control the desert's magic through dark pacts with genies or demons, but few who seek such power return with their souls intact.

title: The Ivory Citadel
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Ivory Citadel stands as a beacon of magic and knowledge, its white marble walls and crystalline towers radiating ancient power and wisdom revered by the Blue Moon Clan for centuries. Beyond its splendor lies a vast library of magical tomes and artifacts dating back to the world's dawn. Every chamber holds wonders—ancient scrolls, mystical relics, and secrets guarded by the oldest and wisest members of the clan who have devoted their lives to the pursuit of knowledge.
historyInfo2: At the Citadel's heart, in its deepest chamber, lies the Eye of Anaya—a legendary artifact of immense power whose exact location is known only to the Clan's eldest. Said to grant ultimate power to the worthy and terrible destruction to the corrupt, the Eye is the reason the Citadel was built. Though a place of wonder, the Citadel is not without danger: some visitors have been driven mad by the power they encountered, while others sought to steal its secrets only to face the wrath of its devoted guardians.

title: The Market of the Thousand Lights
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: In the heart of Anathol lies the Thousand Lights Market, a place where the mundane and magical intertwine. Thousands of colorful lanterns cast a warm, inviting glow that beckons visitors to explore a world of exotic treasures found nowhere else in Alkebu. Merchants from across the kingdom gather to showcase rare spices, luxurious fabrics, and intricate jewelry. Yet the market's true allure lies in its magical artifacts—items said to possess incredible powers, sought after by wizards and adventurers alike, some rumored to grant wishes or even immortality.
historyInfo2: Yet beauty conceals danger. Thieves and scammers lurk in the shadows, preying on the unwary. Powerful gangs vie for control of the market's lucrative trade, and the very wealth that makes the Thousand Lights a beacon also makes it a target. For those daring enough to venture deep, fortunes can be made or lost, alliances forged or broken, and the fate of kingdoms decided among its colorful and mysterious depths.

title: The Dragon Fang Mountains
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Dragon Fang Mountains stretch across southeastern Alkebu, their peaks piercing the sky in an awe-inspiring display of natural majesty. These peaks are home to fearless lions, tigers, panthers, and other fierce predators that pose constant threat to explorers. Nomadic tribes have dwelt in these mountains for centuries, developing unique cultures and survival skills suited to the harsh environment. The sound of their drums mingles with cascading waterfalls and crystal-clear rivers, creating an enchanting atmosphere.
historyInfo2: Legends speak of great treasures and ancient artifacts hidden within the mountains, drawing adventurers and hunters from across the world. The promise of unimaginable wealth and untold secrets proves irresistible to many. Beyond the dangers and wonders, the Dragon Fang Mountains offer breathtaking natural beauty—misty peaks, hidden waterfalls, and majestic landscapes—making them as tempting to explorers seeking knowledge as to those seeking fortune and glory.

title: The Council Hall of Elders
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Council Hall of Elders stands as a majestic sentinel over Anathol, its white marble facade etched with intricate carvings that speak of dignity and magnificence. Within, a grand hall of arbitration serves as the stage for the most critical assemblies of the clans. Lofty ceilings and detailed mosaics narrate Alkebu's history and folklore, while vibrant frescoes celebrate the clans' greatest victories and acknowledge their past sorrows.
historyInfo2: At the Council Hall's core lies the Circle of Elders, where the wisest from each clan gather to guide Alkebu's destiny. This innermost chamber gleams with ancestral relics and treasures, protected by devoted and skilled warriors. An extensive library within the hall holds volumes on history, culture, and magical arts. Yet power breeds corruption: whispers of deceit, political machinations, and clandestine conspiracies echo through these hallowed halls, testing the very wisdom meant to guide the region.

title: The Temple of the Sacred Fire
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Temple of the Sacred Fire crowns the rugged Peaks of Flame, the largest mountain range in Alkebu. Built by the Burning Sun Clan to honor Ethoras and Necis, it stands as a magnificent testament to faith and craftsmanship—constructed entirely of red stone and adorned with intricate gold details. Two colossal warrior statues armed with spears and shields flank the entrance, standing eternal guard. Within, fire priests guide visitors to the sacred altars, where a magical flame burns eternally at its heart.
historyInfo2: The temple serves as a place of blessing for the kingdom's warriors, who visit before war to seek divine protection. Yet mysteries lurk within: rumors speak of ancient artifacts and lost treasures hidden in its walls, and some whisper of dark forces seeking to harness its power for nefarious purposes. The temple is also a bastion against the fierce beasts and dangerous creatures that inhabit the Peaks of Flame—its fire priests skilled in combat and sworn to defend the sacred ground against all threats.

title: The Jungle of the Fangs
group: Places & Landmarks
historyInfo1: Deep within the Black Lion Clan's territory lies the Jungle of the Fangs, a place feared and revered in equal measure. Its tangled vines, murky swamps, and treacherous terrain hide ferocious predators and cunning hunters alike. Among its inhabitants are the Tabaxi, intelligent feline hunters who possess extensive knowledge of the jungle's secrets and are fiercely loyal to those who earn their trust. Yet not all tribes are benevolent: the Yuan-ti, serpentfolk and bitter enemies of humans, practice dark rituals and human sacrifices in hidden temples, controlling snakes and reptiles as weapons.
historyInfo2: Beyond tribal dangers, the jungle harbors fearsome predators—tigers, panthers, giant anacondas, and even dinosaurs—waiting to strike from shadow and swamp. Yet the Jungle of the Fangs also offers rich resources: exotic plants yield potent alchemies, ancient ruins and temples promise lost treasures, and human inhabitants have learned to harness the jungle's bounty through knowledge and magic. For the brave and wise, the dangers are balanced by the promise of discovery and the wonders hidden within its verdant embrace.
