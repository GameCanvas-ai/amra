title: Manajur

callout2: The Royal Family Kajin rules with wisdom earned through liberation and rebuilt with honor for all peoples.
historyInfo1: Manajur's history stretches back countless centuries, born when disparate tribes of fisherfolk and farmers united under the revered Queen Ashaya. Ancient magic granted Ashaya the power to forge a pact with Pyrathor, a mighty fiery titan whose warmth shaped the kingdom's climate despite its northwestern position. She established the capital city of Syan and expanded Manajur through a potent blend of diplomacy, warfare, and sorcery. The Ashayani dynasty presided over an era where magic and nature flourished together—the Flamebringers, sorcerers of Syan, mastered elemental magic and drew upon Pyrathor's power to defend their realm and unlock the jungle's mysteries.
historyInfo2: The dynasty eventually fell into corruption and decay, allowing Banlaya to seize Manajur's lands. In the kingdom's darkest hour, Kaelan Kajin emerged as liberator, rallying the people and waging a brutal war of liberation against the occupiers. The ensuing conflict was protracted but ultimately victorious. Today, Manajur stands prosperous and serene, its magic rekindled, its people maintaining a delicate equilibrium with the jungle's supernatural entities. Adventurers journey here seeking lost magical artifacts, hidden treasures, and the arcane secrets concealed within the kingdom's verdant heart.
constructionInfo1: King Anika Kajin governs with the wisdom his father Chakri instilled—an unwavering commitment to his people and the unyielding resolve to defend the kingdom from encroaching ambitions. The royal family has forged unbreakable bonds with the people through valor, wisdom, and dedication. Rajiv Kajin, the king's brother, is a paragon of martial prowess and loyalty, while Shanti, gifted in the arts, demonstrates the kingdom's creative spirit. Many artists and artisans have found sanctuary and support under Kajin patronage, their talents enriching the realm.
constructionInfo2: The kingdom's leadership encompasses not only the crown but also councils of nobles and sages who advise on political, economic, and military matters. The Order of the Flamebringers—wizards and sorcerers studying elemental magic—remains one of Syan's most prominent organizations. Merchant guilds and artisan associations drive the economy while maintaining cultural traditions. Religious institutions dedicated to various deities guide spiritual life.
locationInfo1: Syan, the capital, sits on the southern shore of Manajur's largest lake, a breathtaking metropolis of cultural richness and stunning architecture. The city was founded centuries ago and is celebrated for its vibrant history and remarkable structures. The ancient Temple of the Four Winds stands as a magnificent devotion to the elements—air, water, earth, and fire. The Palace of the Kings serves as the official residence of ruling monarchs and the epicenter of political power. At the city's heart thrums a bustling marketplace with exotic products from across the region. The lake that Syan overlooks is one of the kingdom's most picturesque and tranquil spots, popular for boating, fishing, and water sports. Annual festivals showcase talent in dance, music, and theater, drawing performers and visitors from across the kingdom.
locationInfo2: Syan is home to the Order of the Flamebringers and numerous cultural institutions that preserve and celebrate elven, human, and divine arts. Beyond the capital lie four major cities—Jarang, a fishing hub on the lake; Bijay, an agricultural powerhouse nourishing the kingdom; Gakh, a warrior city on Manajur's western border; and Móng Tre, a center of innovation and craftsmanship perched above the Endless Sea.

title: Syan
group: Cities
historyInfo1: Syan was founded by the first king of Manajur centuries ago as a beacon of culture and prosperity. The city blends commerce, spirituality, and artistic achievement into a harmonious whole. Visitors encounter merchant stalls alongside temples, theaters alongside libraries, with each district contributing to the city's vibrant character. The Palace of the Kings anchors the city's political life, while cultural landmarks like the Temple of the Four Winds draw pilgrims and students alike. Syan's festivals and celebrations are legendary—the annual Lake of Dreams festival draws performers from all corners of Manajur, where music, dance, and theater captivate audiences.
historyInfo2: Syan thrives as a cosmopolitan center where diverse peoples and cultures coexist. Its economic importance stems from its status as a cultural destination and trade hub. The markets overflow with goods and delicacies; the taverns offer local specialties; the theaters present performances rivaling those of any realm. Yet beneath the bustling surface, political intrigue simmers as noble factions vie for influence. Syan remains a testament to Manajur's prosperity and artistic achievement.

title: Jarang
group: Cities
historyInfo1: Jarang stands as a major fishing center on the shore of the Lake of the Stars, its economy heavily dependent on the bounty of waters. The Council of the Tides governs, composed of leaders from the fishing community. The city's fishermen are celebrated throughout the realm for their skill in navigation and shipbuilding. Fresh fish and salted catches are exported throughout the kingdom, ensuring prosperity and the exchange of local products. The city blends the practical knowledge of fishermen with the spiritual reverence for Odine, the goddess of waters.
historyInfo2: Jarang is built on the bay's shore, with houses and shops clustered around docks and piers. A great rocky cliff towers above the bay, a natural barrier protecting the town from storms and waves. A watchtower crowns the cliff, manned day and night by lookouts scanning the horizon for danger. The fish market lies at the town's heart, where fresh catches are sold daily to customers from across the region. The townspeople are deeply religious, believing the bounty of the sea is a gift from the gods.

title: Bijay
group: Cities
historyInfo1: Bijay lies in the lush basin of the White Serpent River, an agricultural powerhouse radiating life and vibrancy. The city thrives on the skill and wisdom of its farming populace, who have tamed fertile lands into thriving fields of wheat, barley, orchards, and vegetables. These harvests sustain the city and nourish surrounding regions, securing Bijay's reputation as the kingdom's breadbasket. The cultivators are masters of their craft, their knowledge passed through generations and honed to a fine art. The annual harvest festival is a riot of color, music, and dance, honoring the land that nurtures them.
historyInfo2: Bijay has not been without its struggles. Dark creatures from neighboring jungles have begun abducting citizens, their sinister agenda unknown. Despite this menace, the people of Bijay remain resolute and unyielding, their unwavering spirit and tenacity having defeated past threats. The city embodies resilience and determination, standing as a beacon of agricultural achievement and communal strength.

title: Gakh
group: Cities
historyInfo1: Gakh is a fortified stronghold perched upon a hill overlooking Manajur's tumultuous western border, a warrior city radiating unyielding spirit. Its towering walls and imposing fortifications have stood the test of time, serving as a powerful deterrent to potential invaders. The denizens of Gakh are a proud, tenacious breed, groomed for battle from youth. The city boasts some of the finest soldiers across the kingdom, their legendary deeds and indomitable courage echoing throughout the realms.
historyInfo2: While primarily a military city, Gakh fosters a vibrant culture of arts and crafts. Its citizens are master weaponsmiths, crafting coveted arms and armaments. Gakh nurtures a community of artists and artisans, creating exquisite works of art, jewelry, and decorative items imbued with unique cultural flavor. The tales of Gakh's warriors and their valor flow through generations, creating a living tapestry of heritage and honor. Despite formidable defenses, Gakh bears the brunt of regional tensions and remains resolute in its unwavering spirit.

title: Móng Tre
group: Cities
historyInfo1: Móng Tre sits atop a hill overlooking the Endless Sea, a bustling city of innovation and architectural marvel. Renowned throughout the kingdom for its innovative, durable, and advanced building techniques, the city has developed methods of constructing structures using local materials like wood and stone that withstand extreme weather conditions. The city is a hub for architects and builders, hosting workshops and a prestigious architecture school where students refine their craft. Architects from Móng Tre are highly regarded throughout the kingdom as the finest in Manajur.
historyInfo2: Móng Tre is not merely a center of commerce and culture but a place of worship. The Temple of Architecture stands at the city's center, a sacred place where inhabitants perform ceremonies and offer sacrifices to Brom and Maeve. The temple is breathtaking, featuring intricate hand-carved details and lavish decorations. The city's vibrant art and music scene attracts performers and merchants; its picturesque setting and cultural richness make it a fascinating destination. The people of Móng Tre are formidable warriors, fiercely protective of their home.

group: Wilds & Landmarks
historyInfo1: Mount Vajra rises majestically in Manajur's heart, shrouded in mist and cloud, a sacred place the people revere as the home of gods themselves. Climbing to the summit is a holy act, allowing communication with the divine. The mountain is riddled with caves and grottoes used by monks and pilgrims for centuries. Monks have carved pathways and stairways through rock, making the climb less treacherous. The journey is challenging but undertaken by many seeking spiritual enlightenment and inner peace.
historyInfo2: Reaching Mount Vajra's top is an unforgettable experience—standing on the threshold of the heavens. The gods themselves are said to dwell on the mountaintop, and the mist and cloud add to the ethereal quality. Those who have climbed to the summit speak of profound, transformative experiences. Despite the sacred nature, dangers abound—treacherous paths, unexpected storms, and dangerous creatures pose constant threats, but for those brave enough, the rewards are immeasurable.
