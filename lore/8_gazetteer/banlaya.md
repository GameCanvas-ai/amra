historyInfo1: Banlaya's ancient past is haunted by the War of Rakshasas and Asuras—a celestial conflict that raged for millennia. King Kala, the cunning rakshasa lord, made the jungle his fortress, while King Dharma led the asuras from mountain strongholds. The innocent Banlayans, caught between these titans, were forced to choose sides in a war they could barely comprehend. Countless ages of bloodshed left the land scarred, its people broken.
historyInfo2: In the Age of the Warrior Kings, human sovereigns at last drove back the demonic and divine forces, uniting the fragmented peoples under new leadership. From this triumph emerged five great kingdoms: Pala (prosperous and peaceful), Suryan (hardened warriors and blacksmiths), Shanti (spiritual and meditative), Vana (balanced with nature), and Kashi (elegant but shadowed). Though the external threats receded, traces of the ancient war persist—hidden ruins throb with arcane power, some rakshasas and asuras yet survive in secluded corners, and whispers tell of dark pacts between these beings and ambitious clans. Banlaya's present dance is one of balancing ancestral wisdom with the lingering influence of old enemies.
constructionInfo1: Banlaya is governed by five distinct kingdoms that maintain relative autonomy yet recognize each other's sovereignty. Pala, ruled by Raja Kavi of the House of Golden Flowers, emphasizes prosperity, agriculture, and peaceful trade. Suryan, led by Maharaja Vijay Singh, is known for military might and weapons craftsmanship. Shanti, under Maharaja Dhruv Nalanda, focuses on spirituality and meditation. Vana, ruled by Raja Ravi the Wise, champions environmental protection and balance with nature. Kashi, led by Raja Jivam, pursues harmony yet faces shadowy threats. Each kingdom maintains its own government, laws, and military, though alliances between them vary based on need and circumstance.
constructionInfo2: Important figures include Raja Kavi (wise leader of Pala, aged but steadfast), Maharaja Vijay Singh (fierce warrior of Suryan), Maharaja Dhruv Nalanda (spiritual guide of Shanti), Raja Ravi the Wise (nature-lover of Vana, grieving the assassination of his son), Crown Prince Ravi of Pala (heir and military strategist), Crown Prince Amar of Suryan (fearless and impulsive), Prince Arjun Nalanda (disciplined student of politics and magic), Guru Kalidasa (spiritual advisor to Kashi, expert on demonic threats), and Captain Indrajit (military leader and loyal guardian). These leaders navigate the complex interplay of tradition, commerce, and the ever-present shadow of ancient enemies.
locationInfo1: Batad, the capital of Pala and the largest city in Banlaya with 12,400 inhabitants, rises as a vibrant metropolis where colorful banners flutter and activity hums through bustling streets. The impressive Batad Fortress crowns a hill overlooking the city, standing as a symbol of defensive strength and a repository of Banlaya's warlike past. The city's markets overflow with exotic spices, textiles, and handicrafts; beautiful gardens provide calm oases amid commerce. Historical buildings and temples echo the city's rich heritage, while its reputation for exquisite cuisine draws foodies from across the continent. Batad pulses with creative energy and cultural pride.
locationInfo2: Banlaya's other notable cities embody the region's diversity. Gairan, nestled in the mountains, is a mining and forging center producing weapons and armor of unparalleled quality. Samgar, a coastal trading hub, thrives on fishing, maritime commerce, and cultural exchange. Balgat, a high-altitude fortress, stands as a military stronghold built for war, its walls and soldiers legendary. Bandardi, surrounded by forests and hills, is the realm of master archers and hunters, producing bows and arrows of legendary precision. Sacred temples of multiple faiths dot the landscape, while jungle ruins hold ancient secrets and forbidden knowledge from ages past.

title: Kingdom of Pala
group: Kingdoms
historyInfo1: The Kingdom of Pala flourishes in a verdant landscape of rolling hills, swaying rice fields, and expansive farmlands. At its heart lies Batad, the resplendent capital where Raja Kavi and his esteemed court govern with wisdom and foresight. The kingdom is sustained by the diligent hands of its people who have mastered the art of cultivating the earth's generous gifts. Once each year, Jangal transforms into a vibrant festival honoring Ethoras, god of agriculture—a time of gratitude, reverence, and reaffirmation of devotion.
historyInfo2: Throughout its history, Pala has weathered conflicts with neighboring realms yet maintained relative peace and prosperity. The abundance of its lands and an equitable trade policy have allowed the kingdom to cultivate relationships rooted in harmony and understanding. Raja Kavi's wisdom and vision extend far beyond Pala's borders, earning respect across the region. The House of Golden Flowers, Pala's royal family, has guided the realm through ages with steady hands and compassionate hearts, forging a symbiotic relationship between land, ruler, and people.

The kingdom's history, marked by warfare and conquest, has given way to efforts ensuring tranquility and stability. Yet beneath sturdy walls, whispers echo of the dreaded Rakshasa—demonic entities wielding power to twist minds and cast illusions—subtly corrupting Suryan's court and threatening to dismantle the kingdom from within. Heroes must remain vigilant against concealed threats lurking beneath the surface.

historyInfo1: In the sacred Kingdom of Shanti, an atmosphere of peace and spirituality envelops the land like a gentle breeze. Life centers on meditation and contemplation, with many seeking renunciation and asceticism as paths to enlightenment. The Asura, divine yet capricious beings, have begun to manifest in Shanti, bringing their own enigmatic plans and unpredictable influence. Though not always malign, they threaten the harmony and stability the kingdom has long cultivated. Maharaja Dhruv Nalanda and his family struggle to maintain balance, unraveling mysteries surrounding the Asura and discovering how to protect their land from any threats these enigmatic beings may bring.

title: Gairan
group: Cities
historyInfo1: Gairan, the mining town nestled deep in the Banlaya Mountains, is a marvel of both engineering and combat prowess. Its strategic position and powerful warriors have made it one of the most formidable fortresses in the region, withstanding countless conquest attempts. The city is governed by a Council of Mining and Forging Masters elected by leaders of local guilds. The precious metals mined from nearby mountains are transformed into exquisite weapons and armor in bustling workshops.
historyInfo2: Gairan is renowned for some of the land's most skilled blacksmiths and goldsmiths; a sword or suit of armor crafted there is said to be worth its weight in gold. The city's fame for metalworking has attracted merchants, adventurers, and travelers, making it a major trading hub in Banlaya. The market is filled with unique products from across Amra—exotic spices, rare gemstones, and fine crafted goods. As the sun sets on the towering mountains surrounding the city, the sound of metal clanging continues, as skilled artisans work tirelessly crafting the finest weapons and armor in the land.

The city is a military stronghold governed by a Council of Generals made up of military leaders and masters of war strategy. Its fortified walls stand as a testament to skill and determination of builders; the fortress is virtually impenetrable with high solid walls and well-trained defenders. A powerful army resides within the walls, ready to protect the city and repel any threat.

The clang of swords and shields rings from training grounds; soldiers march in formation through streets with evident discipline. Yet there is more than martial prowess: the city is home to skilled artisans and craftsmen producing tools and weapons for defense. Forges and workshops hum with activity—sparks fly and metal clangs echo throughout the city. Despite its imposing appearance, Balgat offers warm hospitality; visitors are welcomed with open arms as people share their culture and traditions. This is a city built for war.

title: The Knuckles
group: Landmarks
historyInfo1: The Knuckles, a jagged mountain range that dominates Banlaya's northern reaches, stands as a natural fortress of imposing peaks and treacherous slopes. Named for its resemblance to a clenched fist, the range is home to hardy clans, ancient dwarven settlements, and creatures both mundane and magical. The mountains shelter countless hidden valleys and passes known only to locals, making them both a refuge for the persecuted and a haven for those fleeing the law.
historyInfo2: The Knuckles are rich in mineral wealth, with veins of ore and precious stones sought by miners and smiths from across Banlaya. Yet the mountains are also shrouded in legend—ancient fortresses lie hidden within, said to be from the age when the asuras built citadels here, and some valleys are forbidden, protected by magic or the wrath of entities that have dwelt there since time immemorial. Adventurers who venture into the Knuckles must navigate both physical dangers and the mysteries of a landscape shaped by the ancient war's destructive power.
