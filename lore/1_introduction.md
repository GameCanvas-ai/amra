title: Introduction to Amra
subTitle: A Continent of Wonder and Peril
pullQuote1Top: Magic and steel
pullQuote1Bottom: exist side by side
callout1: Amra is one continent within the vast world of Eridia, a planet orbited by eight celestial bodies.
callout2: Diverse cultures and ancient ruins dot the landscape, each holding secrets waiting to be discovered.
historyInfo1: Welcome to Amra, a continent of sprawling wilderness and thriving civilizations. From the bustling cities of Aregor to the mysterious jungles of Nivarie and the ethereal beauty of Varinthia, this land offers endless possibilities for adventure. Magic flows through the very fabric of reality here, and ancient dragons have awakened after centuries of slumber. Whether you seek fame, fortune, or simply the thrill of discovery, Amra promises trials that will test your courage, ingenuity, and determination.
historyInfo2: In recent centuries, the nations of Amra have grown stronger through trade, diplomacy, and shared struggle against common threats. The discovery of distant continents has expanded horizons, while the return of dragons has unified disparate peoples in both alliance and apprehension. Yet beneath the surface of prosperity, tensions simmer between kingdoms, and whispers of ancient evils stir in forgotten places. It is in this age of wonder and uncertainty that adventurers carve their legends into the world.

title: Alkebu
historyInfo1: Alkebu stretches across Amra's sun-baked southern reaches, a land where tradition runs deep and the Dragonblood tribes maintain their heritage of draconic pride. The region balances reverence for ancestral customs with the encroachment of foreign influence from Varinthia and the Dead Islands. Here, the spirits of dragons call to those who carry dragon blood in their veins.

---

title: Aregor
historyInfo1: The heart of human civilization, Aregor stands as a beacon of trade and diplomacy. Its capital, Monedo, draws merchants, scholars, and adventurers from across the known world. The Kingdom thrives through its mastery of commerce and its strategic alliances with neighboring powers.

---

title: Banlaya
historyInfo1: Banlaya occupies the western coast, a region where cunning and adaptability are prized traits. Its people have learned to navigate treacherous politics and shifting allegiances, becoming masters of intrigue. Trade routes flourish here, connecting distant lands through both honest commerce and shadowy dealings.

---

title: Castell
historyInfo1: Born from the Capitulations of the Hundred Nights, Castell emerged as a unified kingdom of humans, elves, and dwarves. Governed by the noble House Dawnspear with advisors from both Tel'Dovan and Dhon Toruhm, Castell represents hope for cooperation between races. Yet tensions with Tel'Areth threaten this fragile peace.

---

title: Dhon Kopjar
historyInfo1: The youngest of the dwarven kingdoms, Dhon Kopjar was founded when the Ironfist clan ventured into unexplored lands. This rugged realm combines the mining and craftsmanship traditions of its sister kingdoms with the spirit of exploration and discovery.

---

title: Dhon Mendor
historyInfo1: Nestled in the northeastern reaches, Dhon Mendor is renowned for producing some of Amra's greatest explorers and scholars. The kingdom maintains vast libraries and sponsors expeditions into distant lands, seeking knowledge and artifacts from ages past.

---

title: Dhon Toruhm
historyInfo1: The mightiest of dwarven realms, Dhon Toruhm stands firm in the Mountains of White Ice. Its grand subterranean cities and impeccable craftsmanship make it a beacon of dwarven pride. King Drogan Silverbeard leads from the ancient throne, though rivalry with Dhon Mendor threatens unity.

---

title: Dhon Vondaral
historyInfo1: A maritime power among dwarves, Dhon Vondaral has mastered the arts of seafaring and trade. Its ports buzz with activity, and its merchants have built vast commercial networks. The kingdom balances naval ambition with the deep traditions of dwarven craftsmanship.

---

title: Dead Islands
historyInfo1: The Dead Islands loom to the south of Amra as a place of dread and mystery. Few who venture there return with their sanity intact. Strange creatures and ancient evils are rumored to inhabit these forsaken lands, and they occasionally threaten the nearby coasts.

---

title: Kievan
historyInfo1: Kievan occupies northeastern Amra, a realm known for the bravery and indomitable spirit of its people. They have weathered countless trials and emerged stronger each time. The humans of Kievan are skilled warriors and fierce defenders of their homeland.

---

title: Manajur
historyInfo1: Manajur spreads across Amra's tropical heartland, a land where arcane magic thrives in the lush vegetation and verdant swamps. Its scholars have mastered the study of magic, and its people maintain a spiritual connection to the natural forces that permeate the region.

---

title: Skapta
historyInfo1: The frigid highlands of Skapta are home to a proud and resilient people. Barbarian tribes and clans have forged a code of honor that binds them together despite their fierce independence. Trade and diplomacy have slowly brought Skapta into greater contact with the wider world.

---

title: Tel'Areth
historyInfo1: An ancient elven kingdom steeped in tradition, Tel'Areth maintains a strict separation from the affairs of younger races. Lately, it has waged a brutal campaign against humans in Castell, casting a shadow over the fragile peace established by the Capitulations.

---

title: Tel'Donor
historyInfo1: The northernmost of the great elven realms, Tel'Donor is guided by the immortal Queen Naiadriel. This kingdom shines as a beacon of magic and wisdom, its connection to the moons and elemental spirits unparalleled. Here, elves and their allies work in harmony.

---

title: Tel'Dovan
historyInfo1: King Gaeladrian leads Tel'Dovan with vision and compassion. This verdant kingdom maintains alliances with humans and dwarves, proving that cooperation between races can flourish. Its forests echo with songs of peace, yet troubles from outside test its resolve.

---

title: Tianzhu
historyInfo1: Tianzhu celebrates the arts and crafts with unmatched passion. Its people are skilled artisans whose creations are renowned throughout Amra. The kingdom maintains strong cultural traditions while embracing innovation and discovery.

---

title: Yamato
historyInfo1: Yamato stands as a beacon of spiritual discipline and martial tradition. Its people have cultivated a way of life that honors both the body and the spirit. The kingdom has long harbored coexistence between different peoples, a tradition of respect born from centuries of harmony.

title: The Eridian System
subTitle: Eight Worlds in Celestial Dance
callout1: The Eridian solar system consists of eight planets orbiting a single brilliant sun.
callout2: The twin moons Dener and Iothas profoundly influence magic, life, and the destiny of all who dwell on Eridia.
historyInfo1: Amra is one continent on Eridia, the third planet from the sun. This world orbits within a system of remarkable celestial bodies. Aether, nearest the sun, serves as the temporal reference point for the Amra calendar. Erebus, the second planet, is considered the seat of magic itself. Beyond Eridia orbit Krios, Demanus, Nyx, Eryndor, and Chronos—each associated with distinct principles and powers.
historyInfo2: Two moons grace Eridia's sky: Dener, the silver guardian of wisdom and protection, and Iothas, the red harbinger of chaos and transformation. The elves of Tel'Donor revere Dener's benevolent influence, while they fear Iothas as a symbol of draconic wrath. The metallic dragons dwell upon Dener, while rumors persist of terrible secrets hidden within Iothas's crimson expanse.

title: Calendar of Amra
subTitle: Twelve Months, Four Seasons of Change
callout1: The Amra calendar divides the year into twelve months, each bearing unique cultural significance.
callout2: Major festivals mark celestial alignments, lunar phases, and pivotal moments in Amra's legendary history.
historyInfo1: The twelve months flow in ancient rhythm: Aurielis, Belarion, Caelondra, Dronathar, Erethar, Faerithiel, Galadrim, Helvorn, Illyndor, Jorvathar, Kethryllia, and Luminarion. Each month contains four weeks of seven days, organizing time into meaningful cycles. During the full phases of Dener, the Festival of Illumination celebrates wisdom and healing. The crimson moon's full phase brings the Festival of Battle, honoring courage and strength in combat.
historyInfo2: Beyond lunar rhythms, Amra's calendar marks rare and wondrous events. The Great Eclipse occurs when all planets align with the sun, darkening the entire world in the Festival of Darkness. The Great Conjunction, when planets align in auspicious patterns, brings the Festival of the Conjunction and promises of good fortune. The Passage of Comets heralds change and new opportunities, while the Moon of the Dragons—when both moons align—awakens dragons and stirs ancient prophecy.

title: Calendar of Shadows
subTitle: Shadow's Dawn and Shadow's Twilight
callout1: Time in Amra is divided into two great eras: Shadow's Dawn (SD) before the Shadow War, and Shadow's Twilight (ST) after.
callout2: The current year is 1550 ST, counting from the moment the Shadow War ended and a new age began.
historyInfo1: Shadow's Dawn encompasses the vast span of ages before the Shadow War—a period stretching back to the creation of Amra itself. During this ancient era, dragons rose and fell, titans shaped the world, elves established their kingdoms, dwarves carved their halls, humans spread across the land, and great gods guided mortal destiny. The shadow of darkness grew ever longer during those ages until at last the Shadow Lord himself emerged.
historyInfo2: Shadow's Twilight began at 1 ST, the moment darkness was banished and the world began to heal. In the centuries since, civilizations have rebuilt, new alliances have been forged, and previously unknown continents have been discovered. Yet whispers persist that the Shadow Lord's essence, imprisoned within the Shadow Sphere, stirs with hunger. The calendar marches on, carrying Amra forward into an uncertain but hopeful future.
