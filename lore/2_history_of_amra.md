title: History of Amra
subTitle: Chronicles of a World Reborn
pullQuote1Top: History is written
pullQuote1Bottom: by those who survive
callout1: Amra's timeline spans from its creation in the ancient age through the Shadow War and into the present day.
callout2: Each great age transformed the world, leaving indelible marks on the land and its peoples.
historyInfo1: The history of Amra is a sweeping narrative of creation, conflict, and resilience. From the birth of dragons and the clash of titans, through the rise of civilizations and the descent into shadow, the continent has been shaped by the deeds of heroes and villains alike. Wars have carved new boundaries, alliances have been forged and shattered, and magic has woven its influence into every thread of existence.
historyInfo2: Those who study the past understand that Amra is forever changed by the lessons of its history. The Shadow War stands as the great dividing line—a moment when darkness nearly consumed the world, and only through unity did the peoples of Amra prevail. Today, as new challenges emerge and old threats stir once more, the wisdom of history guides those who would shape Amra's future.

title: The Creation of the Dragons
subTitle: Primordial Fire and Elemental Breath
callout1: The dragons were the first intelligent beings to inhabit Amra, created by the divine will of Beral and blessed by the moons.
callout2: From a single primal force, the twin moons Dener and Iothas separated dragons into metallic and chromatic lines.
historyInfo1: In the beginning, when Eridia itself was still young, Beral gazed upon the vast continents—Amra at the center, with Nivarie and Varinthia beyond—and determined to create guardians worthy of such majesty. From the void of nonexistence, Beral brought forth the dragons, mighty beings infused with the very essence of the elements. The silver moon Dener blessed them with nobility, bestowing metallic dragons of gold, silver, copper, and bronze. The crimson moon Iothas, meanwhile, birthed chromatic dragons of red, green, blue, and black—beings of passion and raw elemental fury.
historyInfo2: The dragons rose to dominance, carving vast empires and sculpting cities of breathtaking grandeur. Yet pride corrupted even these godlike beings. Rivalries between chromatic and metallic dragonkind intensified until at last, conflict consumed them. The metallic dragons, seeing the corruption in their chromatic kin, withdrew to the silver moon Dener, leaving only a handful to watch over Amra. In time, this withdrawal would seem merciful, for the dragons yet in the world would require terrible opposition.

title: The Black Blood War
subTitle: Titans Against Dragons
callout1: When dragons threatened to consume all of Amra, Beral created the Titans to restore balance.
callout2: The war between these colossal beings scarred the land itself, reshaping geography and leaving wounds that would never fully heal.
historyInfo1: As chromatic dragons ravaged Amra unchecked, their hunger for dominion grew insatiable. Beral, moved by the plight of the world, sculpted the Titans from the very earth and rock of Amra, granting them power to reshape creation itself. For centuries, Titans and Dragons clashed in epic battles that shook the foundations of the world. The breaking point came when a desperate chromatic dragon, the cunning Nekroth the Black, opened portals to the Abyss, summoning demons and devils to aid his cause. This infernal alliance overwhelmed the Titans.
historyInfo2: The war reached its terrible climax at Fire Ridge, where the great Titan Aelar fell to Pyrax the Red's infernal flame. With their leader's death, the Titans' resistance crumbled. The dragons claimed victory, but at devastating cost—their numbers decimated, their empires in ruins. Worse, the demons and devils they had summoned slipped from their control, spreading corruption across the land that would fester for ages. Amra was forever changed by the Black Blood War, bearing scars both physical and spiritual.

title: The Creation of the Gods
subTitle: Ten Divine Guardians
callout1: In the aftermath of the Black Blood War, Beral created ten major gods to guide and protect mortal races.
callout2: These gods—Adate, Basaj, Brom, Erio, Ethoras, Nithra, Honos, Maeve, Necis, and Odine—remain the foundation of all worship in Amra.
historyInfo1: Seeing the world teetering on the edge of chaos, Beral determined to forge divine guardians who could shepherd mortals through trials yet to come. From the essence of creation itself, she brought forth ten gods, each a perfect embodiment of their domain. Nithra emerged as the goddess of magic and knowledge. Brom became master of forge and craft. Basaj took dominion over nature and beasts. Ethoras blazed as the god of light and life.
historyInfo2: The other gods followed: Honos as keeper of honor, Maeve as muse of beauty and art, Necis as fierce god of war, Odine as sovereign of sea and sky, Erio as guide through death, and Adate as cunning mistress of deception. As these divine beings took their places in the world, Beral's presence gradually faded, leaving mortals to wonder if she had sacrificed herself to create them, or merely withdrawn to observe her handiwork from afar. Yet through the gods, her influence endured.

title: The Saga of the Dwarves
subTitle: Stone and Steel
callout1: Born from the blood of fallen Titans, the first dwarves were blessed by Brom to become masters of craft and metalwork.
callout2: Three ancient dwarf kingdoms—Dhon Toruhm, Dhon Vondaral, and Dhon Mendor—were established to unite scattered tribes under shared purpose.
historyInfo1: In the twilight of the Black Blood War, as the world still smoldered, the dwarves emerged from the very blood of slain Titans. Hardy and strong, they captured the eye of Brom, god of the forge, who blessed them with unparalleled skill in mining and metalcraft. The dwarves delved deep into Amra's mountains and caverns, carving glorious underground cities that rivaled the surface wonders of any race.
historyInfo2: The wise Torum Stonebeard united the scattered dwarf clans under the banner of Dhon Toruhm in the north. In time, two other kingdoms rose—Dhon Vondaral in the east, renowned for maritime prowess, and Dhon Mendor in the northeast, famous for exploration and scholarship. Later, Dhon Kopjar was founded by the intrepid Ironfist clan. Though three kingdoms, the dwarves maintained peace through trade and mutual respect, their networks of tunnels and trade routes binding them together.

title: The Birth of the Goblinoids
subTitle: Children of Blood and Ambition
callout1: From mingled Titan and dragon blood, the Goblinoids emerged as cunning and fierce peoples.
callout2: The goblins, hobgoblins, orcs, and bugbears carved their own dominion in the Wastes of Orthis.
historyInfo1: The Goblinoids—goblins, hobgoblins, orcs, and bugbears—were born in the chaotic aftermath of the Black Blood War, inheriting both the titan's connection to the earth and the dragon's elemental affinity. They gathered in tribal societies across the Wastes of Orthis, each tribe developing its own customs and traditions. The strongest and most cunning rose to prominence, drawing their peoples together through force of will and cunning schemes.
historyInfo2: When the divine patron Tartum took notice of the Goblinoids, many of the tribes united under his influence to build the great city of Drogmar. For a time, their empire expanded rapidly, conquering vast swaths of Amra through both open warfare and subtle manipulation. Yet ambition bred conflict with other races, and the Goblinoids eventually agreed to a truce that saw them relinquish conquered lands in exchange for the right to self-governance and a place in Amra's tapestry of nations.

title: The Elven Era
subTitle: Masters of Magic and War
callout1: The Elves of Eridael arrived in Amra through a divine portal opened by Nithra, goddess of magic.
callout2: Their struggle against the Chromatic Dragon Empire became known as the War of the Thousand Tears.
historyInfo1: Queen Naiadriel Eglaneth and her court of elves crossed a shimmering portal into Amra, bearing Nithra's blessing of magical prowess. They established their realms in harmony with the natural world, founding great cities attuned to the celestial influence of the twin moons. Yet the dragons soon proved overwhelming, and elven kingdoms fought independently until near-total defeat forced them to reach out to the northern dwarves.
historyInfo2: Together, elves and dwarves forged the Second Alliance, and with the help of resurrected Titans, they challenged the Chromatic Dragon Empire. The war raged for centuries in a series of bitter conflicts, each battle claimed countless lives, and the grief was so profound the struggle became known as the War of the Thousand Tears. At last, at the Peaks of the Draken, the Second Alliance brought down the Great Drakorax and his draconic legions, securing a hard-won peace.

title: The War of the Thousand Tears
subTitle: Second Alliance Against Draconic Tyranny
callout1: This epic war lasted for centuries and cost the lives of countless heroes from three allied races.
callout2: The battle at Peaks of the Draken culminated with the defeat of the Great Drakorax and the fall of the Chromatic Dragon Empire.
historyInfo1: The Second Alliance—elves, dwarves, and resurrected Titans—marshaled their combined strength against an foe that seemed unbeatable. Legendary warriors arose: Elladan the elf, Yavanna the elven archmage, Thorgar Runehammer the master smith, and Liraniel the Forest Warrior. The Battle of Ascalon saw Elladan triumph against black dragons through cunning and magic. Yet at Gorgoroth, Yavanna suffered devastating loss, her grief echoing through the ages.
historyInfo2: The conflict reached its climax when the grand armies of the Second Alliance faced the Great Drakorax himself at the Peaks of the Draken. In perfect coordination, dwarves cleaved through draconic ranks while elves wove devastating spells and Titans brought their immense might to bear. When Drakorax at last fell, the remaining chromatic dragons retreated in defeat. The war was over, and Amra could finally begin to heal.

title: The Creation of the Humans
subTitle: A New Race to Rebuild
callout1: Ethoras, the god of light and life, created humanity to repopulate and restore balance to Amra after the War of the Thousand Tears.
callout2: Each god blessed humanity with unique gifts, from Nithra's intellect to Maeve's creativity to Brom's craft.
historyInfo1: Faced with a devastated world and realizing that eternal beings could not alone repopulate Amra, the gods determined to create a mortal race. Ethoras shaped humanity from elements of dwarven perseverance, elven grace, and his own divine spark. The other gods added their own gifts: Nithra granted intellect and magical potential, Brom bestowed craftmanship, Honos inspired honor, Necis provided courage in battle, Maeve kindled creativity, Basaj granted communion with nature, and Odine taught mastery of sea and sky.
historyInfo2: Humans spread across Amra with remarkable speed, establishing kingdoms and cities, sometimes in harmony with their elder races and sometimes in conflict. Tensions arose when humans questioned their tutors and sought knowledge beyond what the elves were willing to share. Notable conflicts like the Battle of Argonath tested human-elf relations. Yet over time, mutual respect grew, and human ambition proved a force that could enrich Amra's tapestry rather than destroy it.

title: The Capitulations of the Hundred Nights
subTitle: Forging the Triumvirate of Castell
callout1: Through arduous negotiations lasting a hundred nights, the three great elven kingdoms agreed to grant humans eastern lands.
callout2: The House Dawnspear was chosen to govern Castell, with Masters of Magic and Defenses appointed by allies to ensure stability.
historyInfo1: As dragons stirred in the Peaks of the Draken and ancient threats reemerged, the regents of Tel'Donor, Tel'Dovan, and Tel'Areth convened to address human-elf tensions. The negotiations of the Hundred Nights were fierce—Arathiel Aelarion of Tel'Areth opposed any cession of land, while Gaeladrian of Tel'Dovan saw opportunity in human expansion, and Naiadriel of Tel'Donor sought compromise.
historyInfo2: The eventual accord established the kingdom of Castell, ruled by the noble House Dawnspear under the guidance of the Triumvirate—a council pairing human nobles with elven Masters of Magic and dwarven Masters of Defenses from Dhon Toruhm. Though Tel'Areth refused to recognize the treaty, the other powers forged a lasting alliance. Castell flourished under this unprecedented cooperation between three races, becoming a beacon of what unity could accomplish.

title: The Arrival of the Gnomes
subTitle: A City Falls from the Sky
callout1: A catastrophic arcane accident transported the entire gnome city of Tinkertown into Amra's Aregor region.
callout2: The ingenious gnomes quickly adapted, becoming invaluable contributors to Amra's culture and commerce.
historyInfo1: On a distant world, the brilliant gnome inventor Daxel Quicksprocket and his team created the Arcane Nexus—an artifact meant to revolutionize magical engineering. When the device was activated, magical energies spiraled catastrophically out of control, tearing open the fabric of reality itself. Tinkertown—an entire city—materialized in Aregor, disrupting the land and creating immediate chaos.
historyInfo2: Daxel devoted his remaining years to understanding the accident and seeking ways to undo the damage. Though he never found a path home, his research advanced magical understanding profoundly. The gnomes of Tinkertown, displaying characteristic resourcefulness and optimism, rebuilt their city and integrated into Amran society. Under the leadership of visionary figures like Glimdor Boggleknob and the Council of the Nut, Tinkertown became a vital hub of innovation and commerce, proving that even catastrophe could birth opportunity.

title: The Shadow War
subTitle: Darkness Rises and Falls
callout1: From the deepest pits of the earth rose the Shadow Lord, an ancient being of pure malevolence.
callout2: The Grand Alliance of all Amra's peoples, led by heroes like Lady Aurana Sunsorrow and Andron Trelianis, defeated the Shadow Lord at terrible cost.
historyInfo1: In Amra's darkest hour, a being of unspeakable might emerged from the void—the Shadow Lord. His corruption spread like a plague, turning mortals into twisted servants and spreading despair across the continent. Yet in the face of annihilation, the peoples of Amra set aside ancient rivalries. The Grand Alliance united dwarves, elves, humans, gnomes, and other races under a single banner. Heroes rose: Lady Aurana Sunsorrow with her tactical brilliance, Andron Trelianis with his arcane mastery, Gromnir Embervault with his steadfast faith, and Evanna Quicksilver with her dexterous cunning.
historyInfo2: The climactic battle unfolded beneath the shattered remnants of Tel'aran, where the Grand Alliance's combined might finally brought down the Shadow Lord. Yet victory came at tremendous sacrifice—countless lives were lost, and the land itself bore terrible scars. The Shadow Lord's essence was bound into the Shadow Sphere, a relic hidden away to ensure his power could never again threaten Amra. With the darkness banished, a new era began, and all of Amra dated its calendar from that moment: Year 1 Shadow's Twilight.

title: The Discovery of Nivarie and Varinthia
subTitle: Horizons Expand
callout1: Brave explorers discovered two vast continents previously unknown to Amra, expanding understanding of the world.
callout2: Nivarie's perilous jungles and Varinthia's elemental splendor offered both danger and opportunity.
historyInfo1: As Amra recovered from the Shadow War, adventurers and explorers began to venture beyond the known world. To the southeast, they discovered Nivarie—a land of dense jungles, gargantuan creatures, and ancient ruins of lost civilizations. Scholars and adventurers were drawn to uncover the mysteries hidden within, though many never returned. To the west lay Varinthia, a continent of breathtaking beauty where elemental beings and fey creatures dwelt in lands touched by primordial power.
historyInfo2: Contact with these distant lands transformed Amra. New trade routes flourished, exotic goods and knowledge flowed freely, and understanding of the wider world deepened. The discovery of Nivarie's arcane treasures and Varinthia's elemental mysteries influenced magical practice and philosophy. Alliances formed between Amran nations and the peoples of these lands, creating a web of connection that would define the age to come.

title: The Return of the Dragons
subTitle: Ancient Guardians Reawaken
callout1: The revelation that dragons had never truly left Amra—merely hidden—shook the foundations of all societies.
callout2: Metallic dragons emerged from their sanctuaries to confront chromatic brethren, becoming allies and teachers to mortals.
historyInfo1: When adventurers discovered the ancient golden dragon Itharion concealed in King's Crown mountains, they unwittingly triggered a cascade of change. Itharion's revelation that dragons had lived in secret throughout Amra's history, and that metallic dragons had been waiting on the moon Dener for the right moment, sent shockwaves through every kingdom. Even more troubling, the news awakened the chromatic dragons, who emerged from their hidden lairs with renewed hunger for dominion.
historyInfo2: Desperate nations sought alliance with the noble metallic dragons, who agreed to return to Amra to protect their world from chromatic corruption. The dragons' return brought both hope and danger—their battles with chromatic kin carved new scars across the land, yet their wisdom and power proved invaluable. The dragons began to teach select mortals the secrets of magic, forging bonds between races that would define Amra's future. The existence of dragons became an undeniable part of daily life, transforming how mortals understood their world.

title: Present Time
subTitle: An Age of Uncertainty and Promise
callout1: Amra now stands at a crossroads, with kingdoms unified in some areas but tensions simmering in others.
callout2: Dragons walk openly among mortals, distant continents remain partially explored, and ancient evils stir in forgotten places.
historyInfo1: In the present age, Amra thrives with trade and cultural exchange. Aregor's capital Monedo sparkles as a beacon of commerce and culture. The elven kingdoms Tel'Donor and Tel'Dovan flourish in unity with their allies, while Dhon Toruhm stands firm beneath the Mountains of White Ice. Yet storm clouds gather on the horizon—Tel'Areth wages war against Castell, tensions roil between western kingdoms, and whispers suggest the Shadow War's darkness grows louder still.
historyInfo2: The return of dragons has fundamentally altered how mortals perceive their place in the world. Heroes and adventurers continue to explore the farthest reaches of Amra, brave the lairs of chromatic dragons, and uncover ancient mysteries. For every triumph achieved through cooperation between races, new challenges emerge to test that unity. The future of Amra remains unwritten, waiting for those brave or foolish enough to seize it and shape it with their own hands.
