title: Gods and Religions of Amra
subTitle: The Divine Pantheon

callout1: Ten major deities—born from the will of Beral—serve as the foundation of religious practice across Amra.
callout2: Though known by different names in different regions, all worship in Amra traces back to these ten divine beings.
historyInfo1: In the realm of Amra, the gods are both powerful and mysterious, shaping the lives of mortals and the essence of the world itself. At the heart of this divine tapestry is Beral, the creator whose infinite wisdom and unfathomable power spawned the pantheon. Beral's divine purpose was to forge guardians to guide mortals and uphold the balance across the planes of existence. Thus, ten major deities were born, each with unique aspects and powers, destined to intertwine with the lives of mortals.
historyInfo2: These gods are revered with fervent devotion across Amra, though their true natures remain shrouded in mystery. Some believe the gods are facets of a singular cosmic entity that hears and answers the prayers of mortals. Others hold that each deity is an individual wielding their own domain and power. What remains certain is that the gods have guided Amran history and continue to influence the fates of all who dwell upon the continent. The monastic orders of Amra dedicate their lives to uncovering the truth of the gods' origins and nature.

title: Beral, the Creator
subTitle: The Mother of All Gods
historyInfo1: Beral is revered as the mother of the gods, the powerful entity who oversees the entire pantheon and the cosmos beyond. Known as "The Mother of the Gods" or "The First," Beral shaped the world of Amra and created the ten deities who maintain balance within it. Beral's presence in Amran mythology is enigmatic and distant, rarely meddling in the affairs of mortals or gods. Her focus lies in maintaining cosmic order and equilibrium, intervening only when absolutely necessary.
historyInfo2: Beral is often depicted as a vast, luminous being with power to create and destroy entire worlds. Her true form is said to exist beyond the comprehension of mortal minds, and even the gods themselves glimpse only fleeting shadows of her immeasurable wisdom. Some of Amra's most mystical and secretive religious orders claim direct contact with Beral through prayer and meditation, though whether these claims are true remains a matter of profound debate. Despite her absence from direct involvement in mortal affairs, Beral's influence is felt everywhere, a constant reminder of ultimate power and majesty.

title: Adate
subTitle: The Goddess of Lies and Deceit
historyInfo1: Adate is a goddess of paradox—a being of cunning, illusion, and treachery whose very existence testifies to the necessity of balance within the universe. Her presence is considered malign by many, yet her role in maintaining cosmic equilibrium is acknowledged by those who study divine mysteries. Adate is enigmatic and ever-shifting, taking on myriad disguises to ensnare her victims. She is an expert in seduction and manipulation, her talents for subterfuge unparalleled among the gods.
historyInfo2: Her cult draws the ambitious and power-hungry, those willing to pay the price for influence and supremacy. Adate's followers operate in shadows, hidden from judgmental eyes, employing manipulation and deception as their primary tools. Though many fear and condemn her worship, others recognize that Adate's machinations serve essential purposes—tempering extremes, maintaining necessary balance, and providing aid to those whose circumstances force them into shadow and secrecy. The followers of Adate are adept in the arts of deception and manipulation, conducting their rituals in secret, far from prying eyes.

title: Basaj
subTitle: The God of Nature and Beasts
historyInfo1: Basaj is the god of nature, beasts, and the untamed wilderness. He commands respect and reverence from those who seek harmony with the natural world. Druids, hunters, and all who strive for profound connection with nature offer their worship to Basaj. He is a benevolent deity, his purpose to shepherd mortals toward deeper appreciation of the natural world and their place within it. Yet Basaj is also fierce and primal, capable of meting out punishment to those who would defile or harm nature.
historyInfo2: Legends speak of Basaj's ability to assume the forms of powerful animals—a wolf, a bear, or other creatures—to defend the natural world from those who would threaten it. His followers conduct rituals in the heart of nature, forests and rivers and mountains, celebrating the union of all living things. The clergy of Basaj are renowned for their connection to animals and plants, their healing rituals utilizing the curative properties of nature itself. Basaj's followers believe that protection of the natural world is sacred duty, and they work tirelessly to maintain ecological balance and punish those who exploit nature for selfish gain.

title: Brom
subTitle: The God of the Forge and Craft
historyInfo1: Brom is the god of forging, metalworking, and engineering—an emblem of creativity and skill. Brom was fashioned by Beral to instruct mortals in the arts of metallurgy and craftsmanship. From his inception, Brom has been revered as a powerful being by those who endeavor to perfect their craft. Those who seek to excel in the creation of tools, weapons, and artifacts turn to Brom for guidance and inspiration. Brom is passionate in his purpose, kindling the creative spirit within those who honor him.
historyInfo2: Brom is capable of bestowing blessings upon those who refine their craft and punishing those who pilfer his knowledge for selfish ends. He is attributed with the power to heal injuries related to craft and to grant physical and mental fortitude to those who respect his art. Legends tell of Brom's playful nature and his ability to fabricate machines and contraptions that defy logic. The clergy of Brom conduct rituals within forges and workshops, beseeching the god's aid upon their handiwork. Followers present offerings of metal and crafted items, and their work itself serves as a form of worship and devotion.

title: Erio
subTitle: The God of Death and Decay
historyInfo1: Erio is a somber and enigmatic deity who reigns over death, oblivion, decay, and disease. He was birthed by Beral to guide the souls of the living through the veil of death into the realm that lies beyond. Erio casts a shadowy and terrifying figure, whose dominion over death and disease instills terror in mortal hearts. Yet his role is essential—he oversees the judgment of departed souls and determines their eternal fate, granting them respite in paradise or condemning them to darkness.
historyInfo2: Erio's followers are few in number, often seen as solitary and shadowy figures who have embraced the inevitability of death and decay. Though his cult is small and enigmatic, Erio's disciples are believed to possess unparalleled understanding of death's mysteries. They offer solace and wisdom to those grappling with loss and suffering. The clergy of Erio are primarily priests and priestesses who have accepted death's finality and embraced their roles as caretakers of the dying. Their rituals, conducted in cemeteries and catacombs, ease the passage of souls from the living world to the realm beyond.

title: Ethoras
subTitle: The God of Light, Life, and Crops
historyInfo1: Ethoras is a radiant figure of unparalleled beauty, god of light, creation, healing, and agriculture. His very presence illuminates hearts, and his power brings forth healing and prosperity. Ethoras blessed the earth with fertility and abundant crops, nurturing the land and its denizens. He is said to have crafted the first rays of sun and stars, celestial guides that lead the souls of the deceased into the afterlife. Furthermore, he is revered as the creator of life itself—vegetation, animals, and all that flourishes and thrives.
historyInfo2: The clergy of Ethoras are devoted to worship and gratitude for the divine gifts of light and life. Their rituals, often held amid verdant fields or places of natural beauty, celebrate the god's benevolence. Followers believe that divine light can heal all maladies and perform ceremonies and prayers for the sick and injured. However, in the region of Castell, the once-noble cult of Ethoras has become corrupted by extremism. Zealous inquisitors have perverted the god's teachings, using his symbol as a weapon of oppression rather than a beacon of hope. Unbeknownst to these inquisitors, they have lost Ethoras's favor, and many faithful work in secret to reclaim the true teachings of their god.

title: Nithra
subTitle: The Goddess of Magic and Knowledge
historyInfo1: Nithra is the enigmatic goddess of magic, knowledge, science, the moons, and the night. Born from the unfathomable wisdom of Beral, Nithra presides over all mysteries of the arcane and intellectual domains. She is a figure embodying both sagacity and capriciousness, casting her ethereal gaze across the boundless reaches of Amra. As the wellspring of all magic, knowledge, and science, her benedictions are ardently sought by those who hunger for arcane mastery or intellectual prowess. Holding dominion over the night and the moons, Nithra orchestrates the celestial ballet of the stars and the mysterious emergence of eclipses.
historyInfo2: Her symbol—a crescent moon tenderly embracing a shadowed orb—adorns the sanctuaries of her hallowed temples. Many hold that Nithra resides within the penumbra, unveiling her illuminating insights only to those who have achieved true enlightenment. Nithra's clergy are erudite wizards and sages who plumb the depths of magic's mysteries and the vast expanse of the cosmos. Their rituals are intricate and multifarious, intertwining magical spells into elaborate ceremonies. They meditate beneath the moon's resplendent glow, embracing the lunar radiance to fortify their connection with the goddess.

title: Honos
subTitle: The God of Honor and Honesty
historyInfo1: Honos is the god of unwavering devotion and eternal vigilance, personifying the essence of duty, veracity, and honor. He is esteemed as the protector of sentinels, valiant warriors, and all who swear fealty to the defense of the vulnerable. His sacred symbol—a steadfast shield emblazoned with a crossing sword—exemplifies his unwavering commitment to the principles of honor and guardianship.
historyInfo2: Honos inspires his disciples to embody roles as pillars of justice, advocates for the downtrodden, and caretakers of purity. As the divine steward of honor and unblemished integrity, Honos demands that his followers walk paths of sincerity and fairness, eternally striving toward moral righteousness. His contempt for the webs of deceit and treachery is unrivaled. Though his flock pales in comparison to those of his celestial brethren, Honos's influence stretches far and wide, reaching into the deepest corners of Amra. Even those who do not explicitly profess faith in him may find themselves graced by his benevolent guidance.

title: Maeve
subTitle: The Goddess of Music, Beauty, and Art
historyInfo1: Maeve is the goddess of music, the arts, beauty, passion, and desire. She was brought into being by Beral to ignite creativity and passion in the souls of her followers. Artists, musicians, poets, and lovers across Amra worship Maeve, drawn to her beauty, charm, and irresistible allure. Her followers are believed to be blessed with divine artistic talent and a burning passion unmatched by any other god.
historyInfo2: While Maeve is undoubtedly associated with physical beauty and romantic passion, she is also the goddess of peace and harmony. Her depiction in art always features a golden lyre, symbolizing her love of music and the arts. Maeveites believe that beauty is a divine force that should be celebrated and honored, and that creating and appreciating art is a pathway to divinity. They hold that beauty can unite people and foster harmony across differences. The Church of Maeve is structured around temples and shrines designed to inspire worship of beauty. Priests and priestesses, known as Bearers of Beauty, are chosen for their outstanding artistic talents and abilities to inspire devotion.

title: Necis
subTitle: The God of War and Conquest
historyInfo1: Necis is a god of war and strife who revels in carnage and subjugation. Born of the same divine creator as the other gods, Necis was the first to possess a heart tainted with hatred and ambition. From the outset, Necis craved dominance and authority, teaching mortals the art of forging weapons and the ways of conquest. He instilled in them the belief that violence and intimidation are instruments of power.
historyInfo2: The church of Necis stands upon the tenets of war and brute force, with adherents convinced that power and glory can only be attained through bloodshed. The rites of Necis's worshippers are savage and merciless, frequently encompassing duels to the death and blood offerings. His temples are erected around fortresses and castles, and followers believe that Necis's presence safeguards these edifices. Though Necis commands respect and fear, many regard him as barbarous and sanguinary. Yet his followers remain steadfast in their devotion, propelled by their yearning for power and glory through battle.

title: Odine
subTitle: The Goddess of Sea and Sky
historyInfo1: Odine is the serene and majestic goddess who emerged from the divine hand of Beral to govern the elemental forces of the sea and the sky. She serves as a beacon of hope for the seafaring folk and fishermen who rely on the ocean's bounty for sustenance. Her very essence embodies the balance existing between the fathomless depths of the ocean and the boundless expanse of the sky. It is believed that she holds sway over an enchanted underwater kingdom where she commands the winds and tides, bestowing rain or sunshine as needed.
historyInfo2: Legend has it that Odine's presence can quell even the most tempestuous storms, offering comfort and protection to those in dire straits. Yet despite her gentle nature, Odine's feral aspect symbolizes the destructive powers of the sea and the sky, and her name is invoked both in gratitude for calm seas and as a ward against the fury of storms. The clerics of Odine don amulets bearing the emblem of sea waves during their sacred rituals as testament to their devotion. Throughout the year, her clergy engage in numerous rites—from baptismal ceremonies for children of seafaring families to the Festival of Storms celebrated on the winter solstice.
