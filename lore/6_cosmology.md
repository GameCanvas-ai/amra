title: Cosmology of Amra
subTitle: The Great Wheel and the Planes
pullQuote1Top: All planes
pullQuote1Bottom: are connected
callout1: Amra exists at the center of the Great Wheel, surrounded by transitive and elemental planes.
callout2: Both higher and lower planes exist in balance around the material world, influencing the fates of mortals.
historyInfo1: Amra's cosmology is a complex and intricate web of planes and realms, all interconnected and existing in harmony with one another. At the heart of this cosmology is the Great Wheel, a divine structure that stretches from the deepest, darkest Abyss to the highest, brightest heavenly realms. At the center of the Great Wheel is the mortal world of Amra, surrounded by lower planes of infernal dread and upper planes of celestial light.
historyInfo2: The lower planes are the infernal realms, the Abyss, and the Infinite Darkness, where demons, devils, and other evil creatures make their home. These realms are filled with darkness and despair, and those who dare to venture into them risk being consumed by the corruption and malevolence that infests them. The higher planes are the celestial realms and the Eternal Wisdom, where angels, gods, and other benevolent beings reside. These realms are filled with light and goodness, and those who journey there may be blessed with divine wisdom and heavenly grace.

title: Shadow Plane
subTitle: The Realm of Darkness and Decay
callout1: The Shadow Plane is a twisted reflection of the material world, filled with darkness and negative emotions manifest.
callout2: Erio, god of death, exerts special influence over this shadowy dimension.
historyInfo1: The Shadow Plane is a place of darkness and despair, where light barely penetrates and where negative emotions and dark thoughts manifest in physical form. It spans all of Amra, twisted into a shadowy reflection of the material world, shifted and distorted by the darkness that dwells there. It is a dangerous and threatening place where the dead and spirits roam, and adventurers must be careful when venturing into it, for both physical and spiritual perils abound.
historyInfo2: The Shadow Plane is intimately connected to death and decay. Erio, the god of death, exerts his influence over this realm in special and profound ways. The boundaries between the material plane and the Shadow Plane are thin in places of death—graveyards, battlefields, and tombs—and crossings between the worlds are possible for those with knowledge and power. Those who dwell or travel in the Shadow Plane often find their very essence slowly consumed by the darkness, and many who venture too deeply are lost forever.

title: Faerie Plane
subTitle: The Realm of Enchantment and Life
callout1: The Faerie Plane is a magnificent and radiant image of the material world, suffused with wild magic.
callout2: Nature manifests in magical and otherworldly forms, and the presence of Basaj can be felt amplified here.
historyInfo1: The Faerie Plane is a dimension full of life and beauty, a magnificent and radiant reflection of the material world. Nature manifests itself in magical and otherworldly forms here, and fairy creatures such as fairies, unicorns, and dragons roam the lush forests and magnificent gardens. The Faerie Plane is a place where magic flows freely and where life is celebrated in all its forms. The landscape shifts and changes in response to emotion and will, creating truly alien yet beautiful vistas.
historyInfo2: Though the Faerie Plane is a wonderful place, it is also full of unexpected dangers and deceptions. One must be cautious when traversing through it, for time flows differently here and many who have entered the Faerie Plane have emerged to find that centuries have passed in the material world. The presence of Basaj, god of nature, can be felt amplified in this realm, and communing with the Faerie plane is part of various rituals in Amra's druidic circles. Those who venture here often return with tales of wonder and transformation.

title: Astral Plane
subTitle: The Void of Thought and Will
callout1: The Astral Plane is a vast cosmic void where astral bodies journey among brilliant stars.
callout2: This transitive plane allows travelers to navigate between different planes of existence through will and consciousness.
historyInfo1: The Astral Plane is a vast, cosmic void where astral bodies and explorers journey, floating among brilliant stars in the deep darkness. It is a plane of pure energy and abstract thought, where ideas and concepts can manifest in tangible forms. Here, travelers can navigate the different planes of existence, using their will to move through the vast emptiness of space. The Astral Plane serves as a bridge between the material world and more distant planes, allowing those with sufficient power and knowledge to traverse the cosmos.
historyInfo2: Traveling the Astral Plane requires mastery of mental discipline and understanding of one's own will. The plane reflects the thoughts and emotions of those who traverse it, and careless travelers can find themselves lost in manifestations of their own subconscious fears or desires. Yet for those trained in astral travel, the Astral Plane offers pathways to knowledge and experience beyond mortal understanding.

title: Ethereal Plane
subTitle: The Mirror Between Worlds
callout1: The Ethereal Plane is a mystical dimension that overlaps with the material world, providing access to hidden spaces.
callout2: Travelers describe it as a misty realm where physical objects appear ghostly and beings seem partially transparent.
historyInfo1: The Ethereal Plane is a closer reflection of the material world, acting as a kind of "shadow" or "mirror" of physical reality. It is a mystical dimension that overlaps with the material plane, providing access to "ethereal" spaces that exist in the physical world but are not visible or occupiable in their normal state. The Ethereal Plane is a gateway to hidden places and secret locations, allowing those who know how to navigate it to bypass physical barriers and obstacles.
historyInfo2: Travelers to the Ethereal Plane often describe it as a misty realm, where physical objects take on ghostly forms and beings appear partially transparent. Time moves differently here, and distances are not always what they seem in the material world. The Ethereal Plane can be used for travel and exploration, but it is also inhabited by creatures and entities that dwell in the spaces between worlds. Without proper guidance or knowledge, travelers can become lost or trapped, unable to return to the material world.

title: Elemental Planes
subTitle: The Domain of Primal Forces
callout1: Four elemental planes embody the fundamental forces of fire, water, earth, and air.
callout2: These planes interacted with each other during creation, giving rise to the Ethereal Plane at their intersections.
historyInfo1: In the beginning, when Beral created the Primal Titans, she also designed the elemental planes to be the building blocks of creation. These planes are the homes of the most fundamental elements in the universe, and the Titans and Elementals born from them are the purest expressions of their respective elements. The Plane of Fire blazes with flames and molten lava, with towering volcanoes and fiery creatures such as salamanders and efreeti. Here, the very air is hot and dry, and the ground is scorched and blackened.
historyInfo2: The Plane of Water is a vast and unknown ocean with unfathomable depths, inhabited by creatures such as Nereids, tritons, and water elementals. The Plane of Air is a place of constant movement, with swirling gusts of wind carrying everything with them. The Plane of Earth is a place of mountains and caverns, with great underground networks and deep, dark chasms. When two elemental planes meet, a place of transition is created—a strange and mysterious landscape where the laws of physics and magic often differ from the material world.

title: Exterior Planes
subTitle: The Celestial and Infernal Realms
callout1: The outer planes represent the extremes of morality, alignment, and cosmic principle.
callout2: From Mount Celestia to the Nine Hells, the outer planes are home to gods, angels, demons, and devils.
historyInfo1: Beyond the elemental planes lie the exterior planes—realms that represent the ultimate expressions of cosmic principles and divine will. Mount Celestia is a plane of law and goodness, home to the most benevolent gods and their followers. The Twin Paradises represent balance and justice, where souls are judged and rewarded according to their actions in life. Elysium is a plane of happiness and peace, home to the most noble and altruistic spirits. The Wild Lands embody untamed freedom, home to the spirits of nature.
historyInfo2: Arborea is a plane of emotion and freedom, home to capricious gods and the spirits of creativity. Ysgard is a plane of adventure and action, home to the bravest and most heroic spirits. Limbo is a plane of creativity and constant change, home to the most imaginative and chaotic spirits. Yet the exterior planes are not all benevolent. Pandemonium is a plane of madness and pain, home to the darkest and most disturbed spirits. The Abyss is a plane of malice and corruption, home to the most ruthless demons and spirits of destruction. Carceri is a plane of prison and torture, while Hades is a plane of death and sadness. The Nine Hells are a plane of law and evil, home to the most cunning devils and spirits of oppression.
