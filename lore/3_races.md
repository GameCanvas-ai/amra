title: Dwarves
subTitle: Masters of Stone and Steel
callout1: Born from the blood of fallen Titans, dwarves possess unparalleled skill in mining, metalwork, and engineering.
callout2: Dwarven kingdoms are built deep within mountains and connected by vast networks of tunnels and trade routes.
historyInfo1: The dwarves of Amra are a steadfast and tenacious people, having adapted to every mountain range, forest enclave, and coastal realm they have claimed. Their connection to stone runs deep—they understand the language of rock and ore as few other races can. Dwarven culture prizes skill, craftsmanship, loyalty to clan, and the slow accumulation of wealth and knowledge across generations.
historyInfo2: Physically, dwarves stand between four and five feet tall, with stocky builds suited to underground living and mining. Their skin ranges from pale to deep gray, and their hair and beards display colors from coal black to red to white. Dwarves are known for their longevity, reaching maturity around fifty years and living three and a half centuries or more. They see trade, diplomacy, and skilled craftsmanship as equally valuable pursuits.

callout2: Elves reach maturity around one hundred years and are immune to natural disease and magical sleep.
historyInfo1: The Elves of Amra are an enigmatic and ethereal race, possessing an innate connection to the natural world and to magic itself. historyInfo2: Elves do not sleep as other races do but instead fall into a trance, remaining semi-conscious during rest. Elves are naturally skilled with bladed weapons and ranged combat, having refined these arts across countless centuries. Their culture emphasizes the preservation of knowledge, the maintenance of ancient traditions, and the careful study of magic.

Dragonblood culture emphasizes honor, pride, and the acceptance of one's draconic nature.

title: Half-elves
subTitle: Bridges Between Worlds
callout1: Half-elves result from the union of humans and elves, possessing the adaptability of one and the grace of the other.
callout2: Half-elves reach maturity in their teens and can live approximately one hundred and eighty years.
historyInfo1: Half-elves are the result of unions between elves and humans, and are fairly common in the eastern lands of Amra. Their diverse heritage provides them with an innate sense of wanderlust and natural curiosity, driving them to explore and discover new lands. With an adaptable nature and affinity for diplomacy, half-elves easily find their place in the world. Physically, half-elves display traits from both lineages—the slender frame of elves combined with slightly more robust builds approaching human proportions. Their eyes range from striking greens to deep blues or mesmerizing combinations of colors.
historyInfo2: In the elven realms, particularly Tel'Donor, Tel'Dovan, and Tel'Areth, half-elves often face suspicion and scorn, viewed as products of indiscretion rather than as individuals worthy of respect. Despite this prejudice, many half-elves possess strong ties to their mystical heritage and long for acceptance from elven society. Half-elves excel at bridging the gap between different races and cultures, serving as natural ambassadors, traders, and mediators.

title: Half-orcs
subTitle: Misunderstood Warriors
callout1: Half-orcs are descended from the union of orcs and humans, carrying strength from both lineages.
callout2: Despite prejudice and discrimination, half-orcs are hardy, resilient, and capable of great loyalty and compassion.
historyInfo1: The half-orc race is one of the most misunderstood in Amra. Hailing from the union of orcs and humans, half-orcs are often viewed with contempt and mistrust in many parts of the world. However, those who take time to know half-orcs discover a hardy and tenacious people capable of surviving in the most hostile environments. Half-orcs have a distinctive appearance with thick, tough skin, pronounced jaws, and prominent fangs. Their strength and stamina are legendary, making them natural warriors.
historyInfo2: Though often marginalized, half-orcs are integral to Amran society and can be found everywhere from great cities to the wildest forests. They are capable of great compassion and empathy, becoming loyal friends and fearless protectors. In western regions like Yamato and Banlaya, half-orc and orc communities have long coexisted with humans in relative harmony, maintaining traditions of mutual respect and cooperation.
